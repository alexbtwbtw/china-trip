## Booking Checklist (Do Before You Leave)

- **Forbidden City** — Tickets must be booked online in advance via the official Palace Museum website. The daily cap is 40,000 visitors; tickets are released exactly **7 days in advance at 20:00 Beijing time** and sell out within hours on weekdays, faster on weekends. You must provide passport numbers for each visitor. No on-site ticket sales. Book the moment the window opens.
- **Mutianyu Great Wall** — Book tickets online at [en.mutianyugreatwall.com](https://en.mutianyugreatwall.com) or via a tour operator. The chairlift-up + toboggan-down combo ticket is CNY 140 per person — a fun and efficient combination.
- **Temple of Heaven / Summer Palace / Lama Temple** — Recommended to pre-book via official platforms or WeChat mini-programs; walk-up is sometimes possible but not guaranteed.
- **Jingshan Park** — No advance booking required. Ticket is 2 CNY, payable on-site with passport.

# Itinerary A — Grand Tour

*Forbidden City, Tiananmen, Temple of Heaven, Summer Palace, Great Wall, 798, hutong evening*

---

### Monday 26 May — Arrival Evening

**Late Afternoon / Evening**
- Arrive Beijing (rail from Xi'an ~5–6h; likely arrive 17:00–19:00). Check in to NOA Hotel Beijing Longfu.
- Take a gentle walk south to **Tiananmen Square** (10 min on foot) — lit up at dusk, good for getting your bearings. The square itself is freely accessible; enjoy the view without crowds.
- Dinner near the hotel in the **Wangfujing** area — lively pedestrian street with food stalls, traditional snacks, and restaurants. Ideal for an easy first night.
- Early night — the Forbidden City requires an 08:30 start tomorrow.

---

### Tuesday 27 May — Forbidden City & Tiananmen

**Morning**
- 08:30 — Be at the **Forbidden City** (Meridian Gate, Wumen) right at opening. This is the quietest window of the day. You will need your pre-booked tickets and passports.
- Recommended route: Meridian Gate → Hall of Supreme Harmony → Hall of Clocks & Watches (separate ticket ~CNY 10 each, worth it) → Imperial Garden → exit via the Gate of Divine Might (north).
- Allow 3–4 hours for a thorough visit.

**Afternoon**
- Exit north, walk up through **Jingshan Park** (CNY 2 each, no pre-booking required) — climb to Wanchun Pavilion for the classic rooftop view looking south over the entire Forbidden City. 45–60 min.
- Lunch at a restaurant in the **Nanluoguxiang** hutong area (5 min walk east of Jingshan) — pick a spot with outdoor seating in the shade.
- After lunch, stroll **Nanluoguxiang** itself — coffee, ice cream, browse independent shops. Weekday afternoons are calmer than weekends.

**Evening**
- Peking duck dinner at **Siji Minfu** (四季民福) — the branch near Donghua Gate has a view of the Forbidden City's moat and is closest to the hotel. Traditional style, excellent quality, more affordable than Da Dong. Book ahead online or arrive by 17:30 before the queue builds. A whole duck (CNY 200–280) is perfect for two.
- Walk home through the hutong lanes around the hotel.

---

### Wednesday 28 May — Temple of Heaven & Summer Palace

**Morning**
- 09:00 — **Temple of Heaven** (天坛). Enter via the North Gate. Buy the combined ticket (~CNY 34) for the park plus the Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests (the iconic circular building) and the Circular Mound Altar. Arrive early to watch elderly locals practising tai chi, calligraphy, and traditional dance in the park — a genuine slice of Beijing life. Allow 2–2.5 hours.
- Take the subway (Line 5 south from Tiantan Dongmen → change as needed) to the **Summer Palace**.

**Afternoon**
- 12:30 — **Summer Palace** (颐和园). Buy a combined ticket (entrance + Suzhou Street + Garden of Harmonious Interests). Walk the Long Corridor (painted gallery along Kunming Lake), climb to the Tower of Buddhist Incense (Foxiang Ge) for the view, then follow the lake path to the Marble Boat. Allow 3–3.5 hours. Grab lunch from one of the park's restaurants or the Suzhou Street area — atmospheric and convenient.

**Evening**
- Return by subway. Evening walk along the **Houhai / Shichahai lakes** — small bars and cafes line the shore; pleasant and mellow on a weekday evening. 30–40 min stroll.
- Casual dinner in the Houhai area.

---

### Thursday 29 May — Great Wall (Mutianyu)

**Morning**
- 07:30 — Depart hotel by hired car/taxi or a prearranged day-tour transfer. Journey to Mutianyu is approximately 1.5–2 hours from central Beijing.
- **Why Mutianyu?** Significantly fewer crowds than Badaling; restored but still dramatic section; excellent infrastructure including a cable car up and toboggan (alpine slide) down. Views are spectacular.
- 09:30 — Arrive Mutianyu. Take the **chairlift up** (the combo ticket — chairlift up + toboggan down — is CNY 140 per person, booked in advance recommended).
- Walk east along the wall toward Tower 14 for the best views and fewer people. The restored section runs from Tower 6 to Tower 23. Allow 2.5–3 hours on the wall.
- 12:30 — Take the **toboggan down** — a 5-minute alpine-slide descent. Fun, worth it.

**Afternoon**
- Lunch at one of the restaurants at the base before heading back.
- 14:00 — Return journey to Beijing (~1.5–2 hours). Rest at the hotel.

**Evening**
- **798 Art District** (798艺术区) — a converted Cold War–era weapons factory turned arts hub. Take a taxi or Didi (~30 min from centre). Most galleries open 10:00–18:00 but the outdoor spaces and cafes are accessible until later. Good evening atmosphere. Grab dinner at one of the many restaurants inside the district — from casual noodle spots to international cuisine. Budget 2–3 hours.
- Return by taxi/Didi.

---

### Friday 30 May — Hutong Deep Dive & Farewell Dinner

**Morning**
- 09:00 — **Drum Tower & Bell Tower** (鼓楼/钟楼) — two ancient towers in the heart of the hutong neighbourhood. Climb the Drum Tower for city views (CNY 30 each). The Bell Tower is free to enter. They are 100 m apart.
- 10:30 — Hire **rickshaw or bikes** for a guided hutong ride through the **Shichahai** area — ride down Yandai Byway (oldest commercial street in Beijing), cross Yinding Bridge (Silver Ingot Bridge) with its framed view of the Bell Tower, and weave through the neighbourhood lakes. 1.5–2 hours.

**Afternoon**
- 13:00 — Lunch in the **Gulou** (Drum Tower) neighbourhood — great independent restaurants and cafes; try a hole-in-the-wall jiaozi (dumpling) spot.
- 14:30 — Free time: final souvenir shopping on **Nanluoguxiang**, or revisit any site you'd like to linger in.
- Return to hotel, pack, rest.

**Evening — Farewell Dinner**
- **Da Dong Roast Duck** (大董烤鸭) — Beijing's most celebrated Peking duck. Reserve a table in advance (essential). Famous for their "SuperLean" duck with crispy skin and modern presentation. Expect CNY 400–600 for two with drinks. Multiple branches; the Nanxincang branch (near Dongsi) is easiest from the hotel.
- After dinner, short walk to catch the evening atmosphere on **Wangfujing** one last time.

---

### Saturday 31 May — Departure Morning

**08:00** — Breakfast at hotel.
**09:00** — Pack, check out (or store luggage if check-out is later).
**10:30** — Leave hotel by taxi/Didi to **Caoqiao Station** (~15 min from Dongcheng by metro or 20 min by taxi).
**~11:00** — Board the **Daxing Airport Express** at Caoqiao Station — direct to PKX, journey ~19–22 minutes, CNY 35 per person.
**~11:30** — Arrive Beijing Daxing (PKX). Check in for CZ673 (14:15 departure). Plenty of time.

# Itinerary B — Hutong & Hidden Beijing

*Skip the queues — neighbourhood cycling, local temples, lakes, food markets, authentic Peking duck*

---

### Monday 26 May — Arrival Evening

**Late Afternoon / Evening**
- Arrive Beijing. Check in to NOA Hotel Beijing Longfu.
- 30-minute stroll to **Wangfujing Snack Street** — not touristy-kitsch, but a useful orientation walk with street food and atmosphere. Try bing (flatbread) and zhajiangmian noodles from a local stall.
- Early dinner at a simple local restaurant in **Dongcheng** — ask the hotel for their neighbourhood recommendation (somewhere where menus are in Chinese with pictures). Your first real taste of local Beijing.
- Rest — prepare for a full cycling day Tuesday.

---

### Tuesday 27 May — Hutong Cycling & Drum/Bell Towers

**Morning**
- 08:30 — Walk to the **Drum Tower** (Gulou) area, about 15 min from the hotel by subway or 25 min walk. Rent bikes from a local shop near the towers, or use the hotel's recommendation for a guided hutong cycling tour (3-hour tours run from around 09:00; operators include The Hutong cultural centre).
- Cycling route: **Drum Tower → Yandai Byway → Silver Ingot Bridge (Yinding Qiao) → Shichahai Lakes (Houhai/Qianhai) → west through neighbourhood hutongs → Beihai Park north gate**.
- The ride is flat, easy-paced, and passes through genuinely residential lanes — stone doorways, old siheyuan courtyard homes, corner shops, and locals going about their morning.

**Midday**
- Lock bikes at **Beihai Park** (北海公园). Enter and walk to the White Dagoba (Tibetan-style stupa on Jade Island) — serene and beautifully proportioned. Wander to the Five Dragon Pavilions on the north shore. Allow 1.5–2 hours.
- Lunch inside the park at the lakeside restaurant, or exit to a noodle shop nearby.

**Afternoon**
- Return bikes and walk south to **Jingshan Park** (景山公园, CNY 2) — climb to the Wanchun Pavilion summit for a sweeping aerial view over the Forbidden City's golden rooflines. Best in the late afternoon light. 45–60 min.
- Walk back through the hutong lanes east of Jingshan — no agenda, just explore. This area around **Mao'er Hutong** and **Nanluoguxiang** is excellent for wandering.

**Evening**
- Dinner in the **Gulou / Shichahai** neighbourhood — numerous small restaurants serving Beijing home cooking. Try a spot specialising in **mala hotpot** or **Peking-style sautéed liver and offal** if you're adventurous, or stick to classic dumplings and hand-pulled noodles.
- Evening stroll along Houhai lake: bars with outdoor terraces, string lights, and a relaxed vibe.

---

### Wednesday 28 May — Lama Temple, Confucius Temple & Dongcheng

**Morning**
- 09:00 — **Lama Temple** (雍和宫 Yonghe Gong) — Beijing's most spectacular Tibetan Buddhist temple complex, dating from 1694. Buy tickets at the gate (~CNY 25). Five halls, each more lavishly decorated than the last; the final hall contains an 18-metre sandalwood Buddha. The incense smoke and chanting give it a genuinely devotional atmosphere, not a museum-like one. Allow 1.5–2 hours. Go on a weekday to avoid weekend crowds.
- Walk 5 minutes to the **Confucius Temple and Imperial College** (孔庙国子监) — quieter, scholarly, and often overlooked. Ancient stone tablets listing exam candidates. Entrance ~CNY 30. 1 hour.

**Midday**
- Lunch on **Guozijian Street** (the lane between the two temples) — small tea houses and noodle restaurants in a historic setting.

**Afternoon**
- Walk or take a short taxi to **Nanluoguxiang** for the afternoon. Go into the smaller **side hutongs** branching off the main lane (Mao'er Hutong, Ju'er Hutong, Banchang Hutong) — these are quieter and more atmospheric than the main strip.
- Browse the independent shops: handmade jewellery, local art, vintage postcards, traditional food snacks.
- Stop for coffee or tea at one of the courtyard cafes hidden behind wooden gates.

**Evening**
- **Peking duck dinner — Local Style:** Try **Bianyifang** (便宜坊) — one of Beijing's oldest duck restaurants (founded Ming Dynasty), using the traditional "closed oven" roasting method for a richer, more succulent duck. Less tourist-famous than Quanjude or Da Dong; more genuine. The Chongwenmen branch is convenient. A duck for two plus sides runs CNY 250–350.
- After dinner, walk along the illuminated streets back towards the hotel.

---

### Thursday 29 May — Local Food Markets & Hidden Temples

**Morning**
- 07:30 — **Donghuamen Night Market / Wangfujing area morning walk** — start the day early in the surrounding lanes before the tourists arrive. Pick up breakfast from a street vendor: jianbing (egg and scallion crepe, ~CNY 15), steamed buns, or tofu pudding.
- 09:00 — **Temple of Heaven** (天坛) — enter from the North Gate (less busy than East Gate). Buy the combined ticket (~CNY 34) and observe morning practitioners: elderly locals doing tai chi, sword dancing, fan dancing, and group singing in the echo wall and southern grounds. Less about the monuments, more about seeing Beijing residents use the park as their living room. Allow 2–2.5 hours.

**Afternoon**
- 12:30 — Take the subway north to **Liulichang Cultural Street** — a restored Qing-dynasty antique and art supplies lane. Browse calligraphy brushes, ink stones, old maps, and reproduction antiques. No obligation to buy; the architecture and atmosphere alone are worth the trip. 1.5 hours.
- Return to the hotel for a rest.
- 15:30 — Walk to the **Dongsi Hutongs** (near the hotel) — one of the most intact hutong grids in the city. Join a free self-guided walk; maps available from the local community centre. Look for the old guild halls and neighbourhood wells.

**Evening**
- **Ghost Street** (簋街, Guijie) — a 1.4km strip of restaurants open late, famous for spicy crayfish (麻辣小龙虾) and fiery Sichuan dishes. Lively, loud, and very local. Perfect for a long, unhurried dinner. Budget CNY 150–200 per person with drinks.

---

### Friday 30 May — Slow Morning, Local Market & Farewell

**Morning**
- 09:00 — **Panjiayuan Antique Market** (潘家园旧货市场) — Beijing's best flea market, busiest on weekends but open daily. Ceramics, Cultural Revolution memorabilia, old coins, jade, furniture, calligraphy. Great for gifts and browsing. Allow 1.5–2 hours.

**Midday**
- 11:30 — Lunch near Panjiayuan, then return to the hotel and spend the early afternoon at **Wangfujing Bookstore** or the **National Museum of China** (just off Tiananmen Square, free entry with passport registration). The National Museum has superb permanent collections of Chinese history and art.

**Afternoon**
- 15:00 — One final **hutong walk** immediately around the hotel — the NOA Hotel Longfu area sits in a hutong grid; explore the immediate neighbourhood on foot, popping into local tea shops and corner stores.
- Return to hotel, pack, rest.

**Evening — Farewell Dinner**
- **Quanjude Roast Duck** (全聚德) — the most historically famous Beijing duck restaurant, founded 1864. Their Qianmen branch is iconic; the duck is carved tableside by a specialist. Traditional hanging-oven method using jujube wood; crisp, fragrant skin. Expect CNY 350–500 for two. Book ahead or arrive by 17:30 to queue for early seating.
- After dinner, a final quiet walk by the **moat along the east wall of the Forbidden City** — beautifully lit at night, atmospheric and uncrowded.

---

**08:00** — Breakfast at hotel.
**09:00** — Pack and check out (or store bags). Last coffee in the neighbourhood.
**10:30** — Leave hotel for **Caoqiao Station** by taxi/Didi (~15–20 min from Dongcheng).
**~11:00** — Board the **Daxing Airport Express** from Caoqiao (direct to PKX, ~19–22 min, CNY 35 per person).
**~11:30** — Arrive Beijing Daxing International Airport (PKX). Check in for CZ673 departing 14:15.

## General Practical Notes

| Topic | Note |
|-------|------|
| **Payment** | Beijing is nearly cashless — Alipay and WeChat Pay dominate. Get these set up with a foreign card before the trip. Most international Visa/Mastercard are accepted at hotels and larger restaurants; smaller hutong spots may be WeChat Pay only. |
| **Transport** | The Beijing Subway covers all major sights. Tap with a transit card (buy at any station) or use mobile payment. Taxis and Didi (Chinese Uber) are inexpensive; always have the hotel name in Chinese to show drivers. |
| **Language** | Very little English outside major tourist sites. Save Google Translate offline (Chinese Simplified) and use the camera translation feature — essential for menus. |
| **Weather (late May)** | Warm to hot: 22–30°C. Can be hazy/dusty. Sun protection essential for the Great Wall. Light layers for air-conditioned interiors. |
| **Connectivity** | VPN required for Google, WhatsApp, Instagram. Download and configure before entering China. WeChat works without VPN. |
| **Museum closures** | Many museums close on Mondays. 798 galleries also often closed Mondays. Plan accordingly. |
