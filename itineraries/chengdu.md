# Chengdu Itinerary — 22–24 May 2026

## Itinerary A — Classic Chengdu

*Giant Panda Base, Jinli Ancient Street, Kuanzhai Alley, Sichuan food highlights.*

| Day | Plan |

### Thursday 22 May — Afternoon & Evening

**Arrive & settle in (~14:00–15:30)**
Check into Lyhn International Hotel Taikoo Li. Drop bags, freshen up. The hotel is right in the Taikoo Li lifestyle district — step outside and you're already in one of Chengdu's most atmospheric areas.

**Afternoon — Taikoo Li & Chunxi Road walk (~15:30–18:00)**
Get your bearings with a gentle stroll through Taikoo Li's low-rise lanes, built around preserved Qing-era buildings with green-tiled roofs. Walk the 5-minute connector to Chunxi Road, Chengdu's main shopping and snack street. This is a good time to pick up any supplies and get a feel for the city's pace.
- Grab a cup of tea or coffee at one of the courtyard cafes inside Taikoo Li — perfect for a low-key first hour.

**Evening — Dinner in the neighbourhood (~18:30–21:00)**
Ease into Sichuan food with dinner close to the hotel. Try one of these:
- **Chen Mapo Tofu** (10 Qinghua Road, ~15 min by taxi) — the century-old originator of mapo tofu. Queue is likely but moves fast. Unapologetically fiery.
- **Houtang Hot Pot** (near Chunxi Road) — highly rated, bold Sichuan broths, ~20–35 RMB/person for ingredients. Ask for the yuanyang pot (half spicy / half mild) if one of you is unsure about full spice.

After dinner, take an evening walk back through Taikoo Li — it's beautifully lit at night.

### Friday 23 May — Full Day

> **GIANT PANDA BASE — ADVANCE BOOKING REQUIRED**
> Since 2026, the Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding operates on a 100% pre-booked digital system — physical ticket windows are closed. There are no walk-up tickets.
>
> - **Book tonight (Thursday):** Use **Trip.com** (recommended for non-Chinese passport holders) or the official WeChat Mini Program / Meituan app. Book at least 2–3 days ahead if possible, but same-day-prior booking is often available for the early slot.
> - **Target the 07:30 entry slot** — this is the best time. Pandas are fed breakfast between 08:00–09:30 and are most active. By mid-morning they retreat to sleep for the rest of the day.
> - **Bring your passports** — staff scan them at the gate to match your booking.
> - **Ticket price:** ~55 RMB per person (adult).
> - **Getting there:** ~30–40 min by taxi from Taikoo Li. Leave the hotel no later than **07:00**.

**Morning — Giant Panda Base (~07:30–10:30)**
Arrive at opening. Head straight to the outdoor enclosures — early morning is when pandas are eating, tumbling, and at their most photogenic. The base is large; focus on the adult panda areas first, then the red panda section. Plan 2–3 hours. By 10:30 the crowds thicken significantly and most pandas have gone inside to sleep.

**Late Morning — Wuhou Shrine (~11:30–13:00)**
Take a taxi (~30 min) from the Panda Base to Wuhou Shrine (Wuhouci), Chengdu's most important historical site — a memorial complex honouring the heroes of the Three Kingdoms era, with lush gardens and vivid red walls. Entry is ~50 RMB per person. Budget 1–1.5 hours.

**Lunch — Jinli Ancient Street (~13:00–14:30)**
Jinli is immediately adjacent to Wuhou Shrine (connected via the Jinli Exit in the red wall — you don't need to exit to the main road). The 550m pedestrian street is lined with street food stalls and sit-down spots. Good choices:
- **Rabbit head** (tu tou) — a Chengdu delicacy, sold by street vendors
- **Sichuan dumplings** (zhong shui jiao) — pork dumplings in chilli oil
- **Spicy skewers** (chuan chuan) — dip-style hotpot-on-a-stick
- **Sweet rice balls** (tang yuan) for dessert

**Afternoon — Kuanzhai Alley (~15:00–17:30)**
Head to Kuanzhai (Wide and Narrow Alleys) by taxi (~15 min). Three parallel Qing-dynasty hutong alleys — Wide Alley (kuan), Narrow Alley (zhai), and Well Alley (jing) — lined with traditional courtyard houses now converted into teahouses, craft shops, and restaurants. Great for people-watching, browsing, and picking up souvenirs. Ear-cleaning services are everywhere if you're curious. Budget 2–2.5 hours.

**Evening — Hotpot dinner (~18:30–21:00)**
Hotpot is the essential Chengdu evening. Options:
- **Huangcheng Laoma** — one of Chengdu's oldest hotpot institutions (est. 1986), upscale but authentic, well-suited to visitors. Classic Chengdu-style broth: rich, fragrant, and less oil-heavy than Chongqing style.
- **Laoma Hot Pot** — excellent Chengdu-style broth, fragrant rather than overwhelmingly spicy.
- Order the yuanyang pot (split broth) and a range of: beef slices, tofu skin, lotus root, mushrooms, potato slices. Sesame dipping sauce is provided.

End the night with a stroll along the lit-up streets back toward the hotel.

### Saturday 24 May — Morning only

**Leave hotel by 08:00–09:00.**

**Light breakfast before checkout (~07:15–07:45)**
Grab a quick local breakfast before you go — either from a street vendor near the hotel or a hotel breakfast:

**Check out & head to station**

- **Chengdu Tianfu Airport** if flying (~45–60 min by taxi to the newer airport, more for peak traffic).

Allow plenty of time. Don't leave the hotel later than **08:30** to be safe.

## Itinerary B — Local & Relaxed

*Skip the panda queues. Teahouses, temples, People's Park, neighbourhood eating, mahjong-watching.*

| Day | Plan |
|-----|------|
| Thu 22 May (eve) | Arrive · Wenshu Monastery · local Sichuan dinner · Taikoo Li evening wander |
| Fri 23 May | People's Park & Heming Teahouse · Sichuan opera · Kuanzhai Alley · neighbourhood stroll · hotpot dinner |
| Sat 24 May (morn) | Breakfast · checkout · head to Xi'an |

---

### Thursday 22 May — Afternoon & Evening

**Arrive & settle in (~14:00–15:30)**
Check into Lyhn International Hotel Taikoo Li. Take your time settling in — Itinerary B runs at a slower pace throughout.

**Afternoon — Wenshu Monastery (~15:30–17:30)**
Take a taxi or metro (~20 min) to Wenshu Yuan (Wenshu Monastery), Chengdu's most important surviving Buddhist temple. Admission is free. Wander the incense-filled courtyards, the cool shadowed halls, and the surrounding neighbourhood of teahouses and street vendors. The monastery's own vegetarian restaurant is excellent if you want a light snack — dishes from ~8–30 RMB, creative Buddhist cuisine.

The street just outside Wenshu is one of Chengdu's most authentic old-town atmospheres: calligraphy sellers, jade stalls, fortune-tellers, and neighbourhood locals.

**Evening — Dinner & Taikoo Li wander (~18:30–21:00)**
Return toward the hotel area for dinner. Keep it local:
- **Dan dan noodles** at a nearby hole-in-the-wall — look for any noodle shop with steaming pots and a queue of locals; they open for dinner too.
- **Zhong shui jiao** (Zhong-style dumplings in chilli oil) — widely available in the Chunxi Road area.
- **Fuqi feipian** — sliced beef and offal in chilli oil, a cold starter that appears on most Sichuan menus.

After dinner, explore Taikoo Li in the evening — the courtyards fill up, the lights come on, and it becomes the most pleasant version of itself. No shopping required; just wandering.

---

### Friday 23 May — Full Day

**Morning — People's Park & Heming Teahouse (~09:00–12:00)**
Take a taxi or walk (~25 min) to People's Park (Renmin Gongyuan). Arrive by 09:30 to get a good waterside seat at **Heming Teahouse**, one of Chengdu's oldest and most atmospheric teahouses, right on the lake. A gaiwan (lidded cup) of tea costs ~30 RMB and lasts as long as you like — refills are included. Order: green tea (biluochun) or jasmine tea (moli hua cha).

From your seat you'll naturally watch the park's daily life: elders playing chess and cards, groups doing ballroom dancing or tai chi, and — almost certainly — mahjong. Tables of mahjong players are scattered throughout the park's covered pavilions. Pull up a chair nearby and watch; locals are generally used to curious visitors and often happy to explain.

Stay for the **free Sichuan opera face-changing performance** on the park's old stage (~14:30–17:00 on most days — ask at the teahouse to confirm timing).

**Lunch — Near People's Park or Kuanzhai Alley (~12:00–13:30)**
Kuanzhai Alley is a 10–15 minute walk from People's Park. Options for lunch:
- Find a sit-down restaurant inside Narrow Alley (Zhai Xiang) for a more relaxed meal — mapo tofu, kung pao chicken, and gong bao prawns are all standard.
- Or grab street food from stalls: rice cake skewers, tofu pudding (doufunao), scallion pancakes.

**Afternoon — Kuanzhai Alley & neighbourhood stroll (~13:30–17:00)**
Browse the three alleys at your own pace — Wide Alley (kuan xiang) has more upscale shops and courtyard restaurants; Narrow Alley (zhai xiang) has the best street food; Well Alley (jing xiang) is the quietest and most local-feeling. Stop in a teahouse for another round of tea. Then wander the surrounding backstreets — the blocks immediately east and north of Kuanzhai have local residential streets with old courtyard houses, corner convenience stores, and retired residents sitting outside.

From here, you could also head to **Wangping Street / Mengzhuowan** (~15 min taxi from Kuanzhai), a trendy-local hybrid neighbourhood popular with young Chengdu residents — hotpot skewers, international food, and interesting street art.

**Evening — Sichuan hotpot dinner (~18:00–21:00)**
Time for the full hotpot experience. For a relaxed, neighbourhood-style evening:
- **Laoma Hot Pot** — fragrant Chengdu-style broth (less intense than Chongqing), approachable for visitors.
- Alternatively, walk the immediate streets around Taikoo Li and pick a hotpot restaurant that has locals queuing — a reliable indicator.
- Order: thinly sliced beef, tofu skin rolls, lotus root, potato slices, enoki mushrooms, and plenty of sesame dipping sauce. Cold Tsingtao or local Chengdu draft beer pairs well.

End with a slow walk through Taikoo Li's evening atmosphere — the lanes are at their most pleasant from 20:00 onward.

### Saturday 24 May — Morning only

**Leave hotel by 08:00–09:00.**

**Light breakfast before checkout (~07:15–07:45)**
One last Chengdu breakfast:
- **Dan dan noodles** from a nearby street noodle shop (~6–10 RMB per bowl) — available from ~06:30 in most neighbourhoods.
- Or a bowl of **hong you chao shou** (pork wontons in chilli oil) — a Chengdu street staple and arguably the best possible final meal before a train.

**Check out & head to station**
- **Chengdu East Station (Chengdu Dong)** for high-speed rail to Xi'an (~3.5–4h on G-class trains). Taxi from Taikoo Li is ~30–40 min in normal traffic; allow extra time for Friday-morning traffic. Metro Line 2 is a reliable backup (~50 min to the station).
- **Chengdu Tianfu Airport** if flying (~45–60 min by taxi).

Leave the hotel no later than **08:30** to be comfortable.

## General Notes (Both Itineraries)

| Topic | Note |
|---|---|
| **Transport** | DiDi (Chinese Uber, app in English) or regular taxis work well. Subway Lines 1, 2, and 3 cover most attractions. |
| **Payment** | Most places prefer WeChat Pay or Alipay. Have some cash (RMB) as backup for street food and older vendors. |
| **Language** | English signage is limited outside major tourist sites. Screenshots of attraction names in Chinese characters help enormously with taxi drivers. |
| **Spice levels** | Sichuan food uses Sichuan peppercorn (ma — numbing) and chilli (la — heat). "Slightly spicy" (wei la) is a reasonable starting request if unsure. |
| **Weather (May)** | Chengdu in late May is warm and humid, ~22–28°C. Rain is possible; a light layer and compact umbrella are useful. |
| **Saturday trains** | Book Chengdu–Xi'an high-speed rail tickets in advance on Trip.com or the 12306 app. G-class trains run regularly; ~3.5–4h depending on service. |
