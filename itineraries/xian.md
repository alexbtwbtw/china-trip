# Xi'an Itinerary — 24–26 May 2026

**Travel:** Arrive Saturday 24 May from Chengdu (high-speed rail ~3.5–4h, or flight ~1h). Depart Monday 26 May morning to Beijing (high-speed rail ~4.5–6h on G-train from Xi'an North, or flight ~2h).

**Hotel:** WALL Hotel Chang'an — micro-boutique hotel built directly against the ancient city wall, in the heart of the walled city. Being on the wall is the defining feature of the stay: use it freely at any hour.

---

## Itinerary A — Classic Xi'an

*Terracotta Warriors, City Wall bike ride, Muslim Quarter, Bell & Drum Towers, Tang Paradise*

---

### Saturday 24 May — Arrival Afternoon/Evening

**Afternoon (arrive ~13:00–14:00)**

- Check in to WALL Hotel Chang'an. Drop bags and step straight onto the ancient city wall — the hotel's position gives you immediate, unhurried access with zero travel time.
- Take a 30-minute walk along the wall from the hotel to orient yourself. The wall is 13.7 km in circumference and offers great elevated views over both the old city interior and the modern city beyond.
- Head to the **Bell Tower** (Zhonglou), the geographic and historic centre of Xi'an (admission ~¥30; open from 08:30). From there it's a short stroll west to the **Drum Tower** (Gulou; combo ticket available ~¥50 for both). Catch a drumming performance if timing allows — performances run roughly every 45 minutes during the day.

**Evening**

- Walk north from the Drum Tower into the **Muslim Quarter** (Huimin Jie / Beiyuanmen). This is the best time to arrive as the street is fully alive with evening trade.
- Dinner: **yangrou paomo** (羊肉泡馍) — Xi'an's most iconic dish. You are handed hard flatbreads and must tear them by hand into small pieces; the bowl is then filled with rich slow-cooked lamb broth. Allow 20–30 minutes for the tearing ritual. Look for established restaurants on Beiyuanmen or just off it.
- Snack as you walk: **roujiamo** (肉夹馍, ¥8–15) from a clay-oven stall — spiced braised meat (pork or beef at halal stalls) stuffed into a crispy sesame flatbread. Also try **liangpi** (cold rice noodles in chilli-sesame sauce) and **lamb skewers** (yangrou chuan, ¥3–5 each).
- Return to the hotel along the wall at night — the wall is lit up after dark and the perspective from the hotel is excellent.

**Practical notes:**
- Great Mosque (Huajue Lane, ¥25 peak season) is open 08:00–19:00; worth a 20-minute look on the way through the Muslim Quarter.
- The Muslim Quarter gets extremely crowded on weekend evenings; embrace it or arrive at the edges (Xiyangshi, Dapiyuan) for a slightly quieter experience.

---

### Sunday 25 May — Full Day

**Early morning: Terracotta Warriors**

- Depart hotel by 07:00–07:15. The site opens at **08:30** and going early is essential — crowds build significantly by 10:00.
- **Transport:** Take Metro Line 6 from the city centre toward Fangzhicheng Station, then transfer to Metro Line 9 to Huaqingchi Station, then local bus 613 (~18 min) to the site. Total journey approximately 1–1.5 hours. Alternatively, catch the direct tourist bus from near the Bell Tower (¥25, ~1 hour).
- **Tickets:** ¥120 per person, covering the Warriors and Qin Shi Huang's Mausoleum Park. **Must be booked in advance** — purchase through Trip.com using your passport details (accepts international credit cards). Book at least 3–5 days ahead; tickets sell out quickly in peak season (May is peak). Bring your passport for entry.
- Allow **3–3.5 hours** at the site. Pit 1 is the headline (the main hall with thousands of warriors in formation); Pit 2 and Pit 3 are smaller but Pit 2 has individual figures displayed up close. The bronze chariots exhibit is also excellent.
- Return to Xi'an city by approximately 13:00–13:30.

**Afternoon: City Wall Bike Ride**

- Lunch near the hotel or South Gate area: **biang biang noodles** (biangbiang mian, ¥20–35) — wide belt-shaped hand-pulled noodles served with chilli oil, vinegar, and your choice of toppings. Try Nanyuanmen Biangbiang Noodles (No. 64 Baishu Lin, Nanyuanmen) or Chang'anqing Biangbiang Noodles near Bell Tower.
- Head to the **South Gate** (Yongningmen) — the main and most scenic entry point to the city wall — and rent bikes. Single bikes ¥45/3 hours, tandem ¥90/3 hours (¥100 deposit). Wall entry ticket: ¥54 per person.
- The full circuit is **13.7 km** and takes approximately 1.5–2 hours at a relaxed pace. The wall is wide and flat with watchtowers, parapets, and views in both directions. Since your hotel is on the wall, you can return bikes to the nearest rental point when done and walk back.
- The hotel's wall-side access means you can pause mid-ride, rest at the hotel, and continue — a flexibility most visitors don't have.

**Evening: Tang Paradise**

- Head south to **Tang Paradise** (Datang Furong Garden) in Qujiang New District. Open until 21:00; admission ¥120.
- This large Tang Dynasty cultural park is best experienced in the evening when the illuminated lake, traditional architecture, and nightly water and light show come alive. The show is a highlight — check current schedule at the gate.
- Return to the hotel by metro or taxi.

**Practical notes:**
- Tang Paradise is a 20–30 minute taxi or metro ride south of the city wall (near Big Wild Goose Pagoda).
- Sunday in Xi'an is busy; the wall bike ride in mid-afternoon avoids the morning rush but also the heat of noon.

---

### Monday 26 May — Morning Departure

**Morning**

- Wake early, pack, and check out. Before leaving, spend 15–20 minutes on the hotel's section of the wall at first light — this is a unique final view of the city most visitors never get.
- Grab a quick street breakfast nearby: a fresh **roujiamo** and hot soy milk, or **hu la tang** (spiced offal soup) from a morning stall near the South Gate area.
- Head to **Xi'an North Railway Station** (西安北站) for G-trains to Beijing West. Allow at least 1 hour from city centre to the station. Fastest G-trains to Beijing take 4h20m; most services run 4.5–6 hours. Xi'an North is served by Metro Line 4.
- If flying, Xi'an Xianyang International Airport is approximately 45–60 minutes from the city centre (Airport Express Bus or taxi).

**Departure tip:** Xi'an North is large and busy — arrive at least 40 minutes before departure for security and platform finding.

## Itinerary B — Food & Culture Focus

*Muslim Quarter deep dive, local street food breakfasts, Small Wild Goose Pagoda, Shaanxi History Museum, local noodle restaurants, evening city wall walk*

---

### Saturday 24 May — Arrival Afternoon/Evening

**Afternoon (arrive ~13:00–14:00)**

- Check in to WALL Hotel Chang'an. The hotel is built into the ancient city wall itself — before doing anything else, walk a section of the wall on either side of the hotel. The view from the parapet looking down into the old city lanes is the best possible introduction to Xi'an.
- Walk down from the wall into the **South Gate area** and stroll north through the historic lanes of the walled city to get your bearings. This neighbourhood is quieter and more residential than the Muslim Quarter — old courtyard houses, local tea shops.

**Evening: Muslim Quarter, first pass**

- Head to the **Muslim Quarter** for an exploratory early evening walk rather than a meal immediately. Enter via Beiyuanmen (the main archway north of the Drum Tower) and wander the side streets: Xiyangshi, Beiguangji Street, and Dapiyuan. These alleys are more authentic than the tourist-facing main drag and give a real sense of the Hui Muslim community that has lived here for over 1,000 years.
- Visit the **Great Mosque** (Huajue Lane, ¥25) before 19:00. One of China's oldest mosques, it follows Chinese architectural style rather than Middle Eastern — courtyards, pagodas, carved wooden screens. Allow 30–40 minutes.
- Dinner: **yangrou paomo** (羊肉泡馍) — the ritual of tearing the bread into tiny pieces before handing the bowl to the kitchen is a Xi'an dining experience in itself. A good bowl takes 15–20 minutes to prepare after you hand it back. Rich, deeply flavoured, warming.
- Follow with street snacks: persimmon cakes (shijingbing), sweet rice cake, and fresh pomegranate juice — all sold along Beiyuanmen.
- Return to hotel along the lit city wall at night.

---

### Sunday 25 May — Full Day

**Morning: Local Breakfast + Shaanxi History Museum**

- Start the day with a proper Xi'an street breakfast near the hotel or South Gate. Options:
  - **Hu la tang** (胡辣汤) — thick, peppery, spiced broth with offal, tofu, and glass noodles. An acquired taste but deeply local.
  - **Roujiamo** (肉夹馍) fresh from a clay oven — available from 07:00 at street stalls. Pair with warm soy milk (¥3–5).
  - **Laba porridge** or millet congee at a neighbourhood breakfast shop.

- By 08:30 or as early as possible: head to the **Shaanxi History Museum** (陕西历史博物馆). Entry is **free** but you must book a timed ticket in advance. Tickets open online exactly 7 days in advance at 08:00 (via the museum's official WeChat account or website — both are in Chinese; use Trip.com as an English-friendly alternative, or ask hotel staff to assist). Daily capacity is limited to **6,000 visitors** — tickets go fast.
  - Opening hours (May): **08:30–19:00**, last entry 18:00.
  - Allow **2.5–3 hours**. The museum holds one of China's finest collections of Tang Dynasty artefacts: gold and silver ware, tri-colour glazed figurines (Tang Sancai), Han dynasty bronzes, and a renowned Tang mural gallery. Highlights are the Famen Temple treasures and the Tang court ladies figurines.

- Nearby lunch: **biang biang noodles** — the surrounding Xiaozhai area has several well-regarded noodle restaurants. Try **Daxian Biangbiang Noodles** on Xiaozhai East Road (Michelin-listed, near the museum) or **Aihua Kuaidai Noodles** (20+ years old, also Michelin Guide Xi'an). Noodles are wide as a belt, chewy, dressed with chilli oil, black vinegar, garlic, and your choice of braised pork, tomato-egg, or mixed sauces. ¥20–35.

**Afternoon: Small Wild Goose Pagoda + Quiet Streets**

- Walk or take a short taxi north to the **Small Wild Goose Pagoda** (Jianfu Temple, 小雁塔). Open 09:00–18:00 (May). Ground-level park entry is **free**; climbing the pagoda costs ¥30.
  - The pagoda dates to 707 AD and is quieter and more contemplative than the more famous Big Wild Goose Pagoda. The surrounding park contains the Xi'an Museum (free, good overview of the city's history). Allow 1.5 hours.
- Afternoon tea or coffee: look for tea houses in the Beilin (Forest of Stone Steles) neighbourhood nearby — this district south of the East Street has antique shops, calligraphy studios, and small cafés in converted courtyard houses.

**Late Afternoon: Muslim Quarter Deep Dive**

- Return to the Muslim Quarter, this time with time to explore properly:
  - **Beiguangji Street** — community-focused alley with small bakeries, spice sellers, and local worshippers going about daily life. Less touristy than Beiyuanmen.
  - **Dapiyuan market area** — a broader neighbourhood market where locals buy meat, dried fruit, and household goods. The best place to find authentic local food without the tourist markup.
- Late afternoon snack: **liangpi** (cold noodles, ¥10–15) from a side-street stall — springy cold noodles in chilli and sesame sauce with cucumber, bean sprouts, and chewy gluten strips.

**Evening: City Wall Sunset + Dinner**

- Head back toward the wall as the sun sets. The WALL Hotel's position means you can step directly from the hotel onto the wall for a **sunset walk**. The light on the ancient stone, looking west toward the Drum Tower, is the best view of the day.
- Walk east or west along the wall for 30–40 minutes as the city lights come on below.
- Dinner: sit-down **lamb noodles** (yangrou mian) or a second round of **yangrou paomo** at a proper restaurant rather than street-food style. Alternatively, try **Xiao's Noodle Restaurant** (肖家面馆, 50m east of Tianshuijing and Bingjiao Alley intersection) — hand-pulled noodles in spiced lamb broth with large chunks of boneless mutton. Portions are generous.
- Evening optional: the **Drum Tower square** at night is atmospheric and the Drum Tower stays open until 20:30 in peak season. A late performance (¥50 combo ticket) is worth catching.

---

### Monday 26 May — Morning Departure

**Morning**

- Pack and check out. Use the hotel's unique position one last time: step onto the city wall in the early morning quiet, before the crowds arrive. The wall at 07:00 belongs almost entirely to local residents doing tai chi and morning walks. This is the real Xi'an.
- Street breakfast near the South Gate: **roujiamo** and hot soy milk, or a bowl of **hu la tang** from a morning stall (many open from 06:30 in this area).
- Head to **Xi'an North Railway Station** for G-trains to Beijing. The fastest services (G87/G88 series) reach Beijing West in ~4h20m; most services take 4.5–6 hours. Take Metro Line 4 from Baishulin or South Gate area to Xi'an North — allow 1 hour total from hotel to station.
- If flying, Xi'an Xianyang Airport is 45–60 minutes by Airport Bus from the city centre.

**Departure tip:** Xi'an North Station is large — factor in at least 40 minutes at the station before departure for security and platform navigation.
