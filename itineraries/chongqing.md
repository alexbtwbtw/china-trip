# Chongqing Itinerary — 19–22 May 2026

**Hotel:** YOLO Eling Art Gallery Hotel, 1 Eling Zhengjie Street, Yuzhong District
**Check-in:** Monday 19 May (~16:00–16:30)
**Check-out:** Thursday 22 May morning
**Party:** 2 people

---

## Navigation Basics

Chongqing is a "3D city" built across steep ridges between the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers. Distances that look short on a map often involve significant climbing, staircases, or long detours. Keep these principles in mind:

- **Your hotel** (YOLO Eling Art Gallery) is ~8 minutes' walk from **Eling Station on Metro Line 1**. Line 1 is your workhorse line — it runs east–west across Yuzhong Peninsula and directly serves Hongyadong (alight: Xiaoshizi), Jiefangbei (alight: Jiaochangkou or Linjiangmen), and Ciqikou to the west.
- **Line 2** is the famous monorail that passes through the Liziba residential building — worth riding for the spectacle.
- **Line 6** connects the main rail stations (Chongqing North / Chongqing Station) and airport shuttle zones.
- Pay by **WeChat Pay or Alipay QR code** at any gate — no physical card needed. Single journeys cost ¥2–¥10. A one-day pass costs ¥18.
- Taxis and DiDi (Chinese Uber) work well for cross-river trips or when you are tired. Always show the destination in Chinese characters — drivers rarely speak English.
- The **Yangtze River Cable Car** (Yangtze River Cableway) links Xinhua Road (north bank, near Xiaoshizi, Line 1/6) to Shangxinjie (south bank, Line 6). Book via WeChat official account "长江索道" to skip queues; on-the-day waits can exceed 2 hours at peak times.
- Carry good walking shoes. Vertical changes of 80–100 m within a single attraction are normal.

## Itinerary A — Highlights

*Classic Chongqing: the icons done well — riverside stilt houses, ancient cobblestones, the world's fieriest hot pot, and unforgettable night views.*

### Monday Evening (arrive ~16:30)

**16:30 — Settle in, explore the hotel**
YOLO Eling Art Gallery Hotel is housed in a restored 1930s Republic-era mint building. Take time to explore the building's galleries and rooftop viewing terrace. The cliffside position gives you a foretaste of the city's drama.

**17:30 — Eling Park stroll**
Walk 5 minutes uphill to **Eling Park** (峨岭公园, free entry). This 1909 Qing-dynasty hilltop park sits on the spine of the Yuzhong Peninsula. Climb the **Victory Tower** for a 360° panorama over both rivers — one of the best orienting views in the city. Walk the bamboo paths and see where the Jialing bends to meet the Yangtze below.

> If the **Eling Skywalk (Banshanya Trail)** is open (a 460 m cliffside walkway linking Eling Park to Fotuguan Park, opened late 2025), add 30–40 minutes for this free bonus. Check at the hotel desk.

**19:00 — Dinner: Jiefangbei area**
Take Line 1 from Eling Station two stops east to **Jiaochangkou** or **Linjiangmen** (5 minutes). Head to:
- **Pei Jie Old Hot Pot (珮姐老火锅)** — a Chongqing institution with 20+ years of history, authentic mala broth, signature large-knife-sliced kidney and gongcai meatballs. Expect a queue; arrive by 18:30–19:00 or book ahead on Dianping.
- Alternatively: **Zhoushixiong Hot Pot** in the Jiefangbei area — fresher ingredients, renowned beef and shrimp paste.

Budget ¥80–120 per person for a full hot pot spread with drinks.

**21:00 — Hongyadong (洪崖洞) night view**
From Jiefangbei, walk 10–15 minutes down to the riverfront, or take Line 1 one stop to **Xiaoshizi**. **Hongyadong** is an 11-storey stack of traditional stilted diaojiaolou houses clinging to the cliff, glowing orange and red from 19:00–23:00. The most photogenic angle is from the **Qiansimen Bridge** — walk across for the full face-on view that resembles the bathhouse in *Spirited Away*. Stroll the walkways inside; the lower levels have bars and river-view terraces.

Return to hotel: Line 1 from Xiaoshizi westbound to Eling (~5 minutes).

### Tuesday — Full Day

**Morning: Ciqikou Ancient Town (磁器口)**

Take **Line 1 westbound** from Eling ~20 minutes to **Ciqikou Station** (end of the western urban section). Ciqikou is a Song-dynasty market town, 800+ years old, with cobblestoned alleys, Ming–Qing timber buildings, incense-scented temples, and excellent street food.

- Arrive by **09:00** to beat the crowds (it gets very busy by 11:00).
- Try **Maoxuewang (毛血旺)** — spicy duck blood and offal stew — from one of the small stalls on the main lane. Classic Chongqing breakfast fuel.
- Visit **Baoqing Temple** at the far end of the main street (small entry fee).
- Buy a bag of **ma hua (麻花)** twisted fried dough — the town's most famous snack.
- Allow 2–2.5 hours to wander at a comfortable pace.

**Midday: Liziba Station spectacle + lunch**
Take **Line 1 eastbound** back about 10 stops and then switch to **Line 2** at Jiaochangkou. Ride Line 2 west a few stops to **Liziba Station** — the monorail passes straight through the 6th–8th floors of a 19-storey residential block. Watch from the windows, then exit at Liziba and watch trains emerge from the building from street level (39 Liziba Zhengjie). Spend 20–30 minutes photographing.

Lunch nearby in the Shapingba / Liziba area — small noodle shops serve excellent **Chongqing xiaomian (小面)**, a ¥12–15 bowl of spicy noodles in a complex sesame-and-chilli broth.

**Afternoon: Yangtze River Cable Car**

DiDi or take Line 2 east then Line 1 east to **Xiaoshizi Station**. Board the **Yangtze River Cableway (长江索道)** from the north bank terminal on Xinhua Road. The 4-minute crossing hangs 60 m above the river with Yuzhong's skyline behind you — one of the most cinematic moments in China. Round-trip tickets ~¥50 per person; book via WeChat to minimise queuing. Cross to the south bank (**Shangxinjie**), explore for 20–30 minutes, then ride back.

**Late afternoon: Raffles City / Jiefangbei**
Walk or take Line 1 to **Jiaochangkou**. The glass sky bridge of **Raffles City Chongqing** connects two towers 250 m up — the retail atrium below is free to browse. The **Jiefangbei Pedestrian Square** is the commercial heart of Chongqing — good for people-watching and a coffee stop.

**Evening: Nanshan viewpoint**
Take a DiDi (~20 minutes, ~¥25) across the south bridge and up Nanshan to **Yi Ke Shu Viewpoint (一棵树观景台)** — elevation 437 m, 270° panorama over the peninsula with both rivers visible. Best between 19:00–21:00. Then dinner on **Nanbin Road** on the south bank before returning by DiDi — try **Qin Ma Hot Pot** or any of the riverfront Sichuan restaurants with the illuminated skyline as the backdrop.

### Wednesday — Full Day

**Morning: Three Gorges Museum + People's Great Hall**
Take a DiDi to Renmin Square (or Line 1 to Liziba, transfer to Line 2 to Niujiaotuo, then walk). The **Chongqing Three Gorges Museum (重庆中国三峡博物馆)** is free entry and excellent — ancient Ba culture artefacts, detailed wartime history (Chongqing was the WWII Nationalist capital), and a dramatic Yangtze River exhibit. Allow 1.5–2 hours. Right next door, the **People's Great Hall (人民大礼堂)** — a monumental 1950s domed assembly hall modelled on the Temple of Heaven — is free to enter at the grounds and courtyard level.

**Midday lunch:** Local noodle shop near Renmin Square. Order a bowl of **suanlafen (酸辣粉)** — rice noodles in a hot and sour broth — at around ¥15.

**Afternoon: Shibati (十八梯) — Eighteen Steps**
Take DiDi or walk downhill from Jiefangbei to **Shibati** (No. 1 Zhongxing Road, Yuzhong District). This reconstructed old-Chongqing street descends 200+ stone steps to the Yangtze riverbank — five themed sections covering traditional craft, food, teahouse culture, and creative art. It opened in 2021 and captures the vertical layering of old Chongqing life well. Spend 1.5–2 hours.

**Late afternoon: Riverside walkway to Hongyadong**
Walk the elevated riverside promenade north along the Yangtze cliff from Shibati toward Hongyadong (~20 minutes on foot, dramatic views). This is a good time to explore **Hongyadong's interior** properly — it is less crowded in the afternoon than at night.

**Evening: Night view from Eling**
Return to the hotel area for your last Chongqing evening. Walk up to Eling Park at dusk for the night-view panorama — looking east you see the Jialing River sweeping around the peninsula toward Hongyadong and beyond. The hotel's rooftop terrace is another excellent perch.

Dinner: small local Sichuan restaurants on the streets below Eling Park serve **suanlafen**, **hongshao rou (红烧肉, red-braised pork)**, and **mapo tofu** at genuinely local prices (¥40–70 per person).

### Thursday Morning — Departure

**07:00** — Final breakfast, pack up, check out.

**To Chengdu by high-speed rail (recommended):**
- Take Line 1 from Eling east to transfer to Line 6 toward **Chongqing North Station (重庆北站)**. Total journey ~30–40 minutes.
- G-trains depart roughly every 10 minutes from ~06:00. Journey to **Chengdu East** is 1h15–1h50 depending on the service. Ticket: ~¥130–165 (2nd class).
- Book in advance on **Trip.com**, the **12306 app**, or at station ticket windows. Real-name passport registration is required.
- Arrive at Chengdu East, then connect via Chengdu Metro Line 7 onward.

**To Chengdu by air (alternative):**
Chongqing CKG to Chengdu TFU is ~50 minutes flying, but the added airport transit time at both ends makes the high-speed rail faster and far less stressful overall. Not recommended unless the flight is already booked.

### Monday Evening (arrive ~16:30)

**16:30 — Settle in, explore the hotel**
Same as Itinerary A: orient yourself at the hotel, visit the rooftop terrace, understand the cliff geography from both the building's vantage point and the hotel's gallery spaces.

**17:30 — Eling Skywalk & Fotuguan Park**
Walk 5 minutes to Eling Park and take the **Banshanya Cliffside Skywalk** (if open) west to **Fotuguan Park**. This quiet park is barely visited by tourists — local retirees play cards and practice tai chi here. Walk the forested ridge paths above the Jialing River for sunset views.

**19:00 — Dinner: local Sichuan near Eling**
Skip tourist hot pot tonight. Walk downhill from the hotel to the streets of the **Eling / Erling district** — small family-run restaurants serve **doubanyu (豆瓣鱼, spicy whole fish)**, **ganbian sijidou (dry-fried green beans)**, and **suanlafen (酸辣粉)** at genuinely local prices (¥40–70 per person total). Ask the hotel to recommend their nearest preferred spots.

### Tuesday — Full Day

**Morning: Danzishi Old Street & Longmenhao**

Take **Line 1 east** to **Xiaoshizi**, then **Line 6** one stop south to **Shangxinjie**, or DiDi to the south bank (~¥20, 20 minutes).

**Longmenhao Old Street (龙门浩老街)** and adjacent **Danzishi Old Street (弹子石老街)** together form Chongqing's most atmospheric heritage district — 18 listed historical structures, a mix of Ba-Yu vernacular buildings and Republican-era architecture from when Chongqing became China's first inland trading port (1891). Far less crowded than Ciqikou and genuinely lived-in.

- Start at Longmenhao: steep stone steps descend the riverbank, murals and sculpture installations appear between old shophouses. Around 30 art installations are embedded throughout.
- Walk south along the riverside promenade 15–20 minutes to Danzishi Old Street. This section has the best river views and the most interesting craft workshops and small galleries.
- The streets light up beautifully after sunset, but the morning light is best for photography — strong shadows on the stone steps, river barges visible below.
- Allow 2.5–3 hours.

**Midday: Yangtze Public Ferry**

From the Danzishi riverfront, find the **Danzishi Ferry Terminal (弹子石轮渡)** and take the public cross-river ferry to the north bank (Chaotianmen area). This is the same Yangtze crossing as a luxury cruise ship, done on a ¥2 commuter ferry with local workers, vendors, and cargo. Crossing time ~8 minutes. One of the most authentic Chongqing experiences most visitors never find.

Arrive at **Chaotianmen** — the dramatic pointed headland where the brown Yangtze and green Jialing rivers converge. Walk up to **Chaotianmen Square** for the confluence views.

**Lunch: Chaotianmen area**
Walk into the streets below Jiefangbei. Try **Hao You Lai (好又来)** for legendary **suanlafen** (sour-spicy rice noodles, ¥15–20). Or head into the covered lanes of **Chaotianmen Market** for fresh-cooked noodles at street stalls.

**Afternoon: Shibati + Huguang Guild Hall**

DiDi or walk south to **Shibati (十八梯)**. Walk the full descent — five sections of old-style Chongqing streetscape — and exit at the bottom near the Yangtze riverbank. From there, walk east 10 minutes along the lower riverfront to the **Huguang Guild Hall (湖广会馆)** — one of China's best-preserved Qing-dynasty guild halls (built 1759), with ornate carved woodwork and a peaceful courtyard. Entry ~¥30. Allow 45 minutes.

**Evening: Nanbin Road walk and dinner**

DiDi back across the south bridge to **Nanbin Road (南滨路)**. This 8 km riverside embankment road on the south bank is the local evening promenade — restaurants, bars, and river-view walkways facing the lit-up Yuzhong skyline. It is where Chongqing residents come for evening walks, not where tours send visitors.

Dinner along Nanbin Road: **Qin Ma Hot Pot** or any of the Sichuan restaurants facing the river. As the sun drops, the northern skyline ignites — an exceptional dinner view. Return by DiDi (~¥25, 25 minutes back to hotel).

### Wednesday — Full Day

**Morning: Ciqikou early + Shapingba neighbourhood**

Take **Line 1 west** to **Ciqikou Station** (20 minutes from Eling). Arrive by **08:30** to experience the town before coach-tour crowds arrive. The morning atmosphere — incense from Baoqing Temple, shopkeepers setting up, steaming bowls of noodles on low stools — is entirely different from the afternoon bustle.

Walk the back alleys behind the main tourist lane: quieter, more local, with teahouses, a small river beach, and old residential courtyards. Exit the town and walk north 15 minutes into the **Shapingba neighbourhood** proper — a lively everyday district with covered morning markets, vegetable sellers, tofu stalls, and no tourist infrastructure whatsoever.

**Midday: Gele Mountain & Zhazidong Memorial**

Take a local bus or DiDi (~¥15) up **Gele Mountain (歌乐山)** on the western edge of Shapingba. Visit:
- **Zhazidong Prison (渣滓洞)** and **Baigongguan Prison** — the Nationalist-era secret-police detention sites made famous by the novel *Red Crag*. Now memorial museums, free or small-fee entry. Sobering Cold War-era history. Allow 1.5 hours.
- The surrounding mountain forest is quiet and green — popular with locals on weekends.

Lunch: bring snacks from the Ciqikou morning market, or find a simple restaurant near the mountain base.

**Afternoon: Liziba monorail + Erling Creative Park**

Return to the city. Take **Line 2** and ride through **Liziba Station** (monorail through the building). Exit and photograph from street level.

Walk or DiDi back to the **Erling Cultural and Creative Park (二厂文创园)** — the old Chongqing Number Two Printing Factory, converted into an arts and independent-shops complex. Your hotel (YOLO) is literally in Building H of this complex, so this is your neighbourhood. Spend the late afternoon exploring: design studios, small galleries, independent cafés, and staircase-street sections that preserve the old factory-district atmosphere.

**Evening: Rooftop + local farewell dinner**

Spend your last evening close to the hotel. Climb up to Eling Park for a final night view over the Jialing. Then dinner at a local restaurant in the Eling / Erling area — a neighbourhood hot pot for two runs ¥60–80 total; the streets around the creative park have good options without the tourist premium.
