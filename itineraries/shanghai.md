# Shanghai Itineraries

**Dates:** Saturday 16 May - Tuesday 19 May 2026
**Party:** 2 people
**Hotel:** The Bund Puyan Hotel, The Bund
**Arrive:** Sat 16 May, flight lands ~12:10 PVG -- expect to reach hotel ~15:00
**Depart:** Tue 19 May, leave hotel by 09:30 for 12:05 flight from PVG

---

## General Practical Notes

- **Metro payment:** Use Alipay or WeChat Pay to scan QR codes at metro gates -- no physical transit card needed. Download the Metro Dadou app as a backup.
- **Payment:** China runs almost entirely on mobile payment. Set up Alipay or WeChat Pay before departure (international Visa/Mastercard can be linked).
- **Navigation:** Google Maps works in Shanghai. Baidu Maps gives more accurate metro/bus routing.
- **SIM / Data:** Buy a tourist SIM at PVG arrivals, or use an eSIM. A VPN is required to use Google services inside China.
- **Weather (mid-May):** Warm and humid, 22-28C. Carry a small umbrella; afternoon showers are common.
- **Metro peak hours to avoid:** 07:30-09:30 and 17:00-19:30.

## Itinerary A -- Classic Shanghai

*The Bund, Yu Garden and Old Town, Shanghai Museum, French Concession, Tianzifang, Pudong skyline*

| Day | Plan |
|-----|------|
| Sat 16 May (eve) | Arrive · Bund walk · Pudong light show · Nanjing Road dinner |
| Sun 17 May | Yu Garden → City God Temple → Nanxiang lunch → Shanghai Museum → Xintiandi → dinner |
| Mon 18 May | Wukang & Anfu Road → Jia Jia Tang Bao lunch → Tianzifang → Shanghai Tower at dusk → Michelin dinner |
| Tue 19 May (morn) | Breakfast · Bund walk · depart PVG 09:30 |

---

### Saturday 16 May -- Arrival

**Afternoon (arrive hotel ~15:00)**

- Check in, drop bags.
- Walk to **The Bund waterfront promenade** (5 min from the hotel). Stroll the full 1.7 km promenade, taking in the Art Deco and Beaux-Arts facades along Zhongshan Road.

**Evening**

- Return to the Bund for golden hour (~18:30 in mid-May), then the **Pudong light show**. Shanghai Tower, Jin Mao Tower, and the bottle-opener SWFC light up from around 19:00 and are best seen from the Bund side.
- **Optional 2-RMB ferry:** Cross to Lujiazui and back -- 2 RMB per person. The most cinematic way to see the Bund from the Pudong bank. Ferry runs until around 22:00.
- **Dinner:** **Nanjing Road East** (10-min walk from the hotel, or Metro Line 2/10 to Nanjing Road East station). The pedestrianised strip has restaurants at every price point. For something more local, try the side lanes off Nanjing Road toward Yunnan Middle Road.

---

### Sunday 17 May -- Old Town & Culture

**09:30 leave hotel.**

**Morning: Yu Garden and Old Town (10:00-13:00)**

- Metro **Line 10** to **Yuyuan Garden station** (Exit 1 or 3). About 10 min.
- **Yu Garden (Yu Yuan):** Classic Ming Dynasty garden. Arrive ~10:00 to beat the weekend crowds. Entry ~40 RMB. Allow 1-1.5 hrs.
- **City God Temple (Chenghuang Miao):** Right next door to Yu Garden. Free to see the exterior; small entry fee inside (~10 RMB). Allow 30 min.
- **Nanxiang Mantou Dian lunch:** The century-old xiaolongbao shop in the Old Town bazaar, founded 1900. Queue early or accept a 15-20 min wait. Ground floor is fast takeaway; first floor is sit-down.

**Afternoon: Shanghai Museum and Xintiandi (13:30-19:00)**

- **Shanghai Museum (13:30):** Metro Line 10 to **People's Square** (~10 min from Old Town). Free entry but timed ticket required -- book in advance via the museum's official WeChat mini-program or website. World-class collection of bronzes, ceramics, calligraphy, and painting. Allow 2-2.5 hours. Closes 17:00, last entry 16:00.
- **Xintiandi (16:30):** Metro Line 1 south to **Huangpi South Road** (~10 min). Restored shikumen (stone gate house) neighbourhood turned upscale dining and bar district. Pleasant for an early evening wander.

**Evening**

- Dinner in Xintiandi or the surrounding Huaihai Road area.

---

### Monday 18 May -- French Concession

**09:30 leave hotel.**

**Morning: Wukang Road and Anfu Road (10:00-12:30)**

- Metro Line 9 or 11 to **Jiaotong University station**, walk to **Wukang Road (10:00)** -- the most famous street in the French Concession, ~700m of 1920s-30s French and Italian villas. **Wukang Mansion** at the north end is the most-photographed building in Shanghai. Walk the full length.
- Connect to **Anfu Road** -- independent cafes, vintage boutiques, small galleries. Stop for coffee.

**Lunch: Jia Jia Tang Bao (12:30)**

- Locals' favourite xiaolongbao, very affordable. 15-20 min wait expected; they wrap to order so dumplings arrive extra hot.

**Afternoon: Tianzifang (13:30-16:30)**

- **Tianzifang:** Metro Line 9 to **Dapuqiao** (Exit 1). A maze of shikumen alleyways with art studios, craft shops, and cafes. Allow 1.5-2 hours. Visit before 16:00 to avoid peak crowds.

**Late Afternoon: Shanghai Tower (17:00-19:30)**

- **Shanghai Tower 118F (17:00):** DiDi from Tianzifang (~20 min). Pre-book a timed dusk slot (17:30-18:30) for both daytime and night views. Standard ticket ~180 RMB. Bring passport -- ID is checked at the entrance.
- ⚠️ **Must book in advance** via Klook, Trip.com, or the official Shanghai Tower website.

**Evening: Michelin Dinner -- Fu He Hui (20:00)**

- **Fu He Hui (福和慧)** -- 2 Michelin stars (2025 Michelin Guide Shanghai)
  - **Cuisine:** Contemporary vegetarian Chinese -- tea-smoked and slow-cooked vegetables, tofu crafted to resemble traditional banquet dishes, seasonal ingredients rooted in Buddhist culinary philosophy. Ranked No. 11 in Asia's 50 Best Restaurants 2026.
  - **Location:** 1037 Yuyuan Road, Changning District. DiDi from Pudong ~20 min. Housed in a restored 1930s villa with a tranquil courtyard garden.
  - **Price:** Set tasting menu from ~¥999 per person (~£110); fruit pairing +¥298. No à la carte. Budget ¥1,300-1,400 per person all-in without wine.
  - **Hours:** Monday dinner 17:30-21:00 (last order 21:00). Arrive by 19:30-20:00 to allow time for the full menu.
  - **Booking:** ⚠️ Must book 1-2 weeks ahead. Call +86 21 3980 9188 or book via Chope (chope.net.cn) / Trip.com. **Walk-ins are not accepted.**
  - **Note:** The vegetarian focus makes this a genuinely unusual experience for this price point. Even dedicated meat-eaters consistently report it as one of Shanghai's most memorable meals. If your party prefers meat-based fine dining, the rooftop at Bund 18 or the House of Roosevelt are reliable alternatives for drinks and dinner with Lujiazui views -- book ahead.

---

### Tuesday 19 May -- Departure

**Leave hotel by 09:30. Keep this very light.**

- **Light breakfast (07:30):** Hotel breakfast if included is the easiest option. The Bund area has bakeries and cafes open early.
- **Brief Bund walk (08:30):** The Bund at this hour is peaceful and has entirely different light from the previous evenings -- a good final 15 minutes.
- **09:30 depart for PVG.**
  - **By DiDi or taxi (recommended for luggage):** ~45-70 min to PVG depending on traffic. Book via the DiDi app (international cards work). Allow for surge pricing during morning rush.
  - **By Metro plus Maglev:** Metro Line 2 east to **Longyang Road** (~40-45 min from the Bund area), then the **Shanghai Maglev** to PVG (~8 min, 50 RMB per person). Efficient if travelling light.
- Aim to be at PVG check-in by 10:00. Hard cutoff 11:05 (60 min before 12:05 departure). Immigration and security at PVG can take 20-40 min on busy Tuesday mornings -- do not cut this close.

## Itinerary B -- Off the Beaten Track

*Hongkou and 1933 Old Millfun, M50 and Suzhou Creek, Jing'an local streets, Sinan Road neighbourhood*

| Day | Plan |
|-----|------|
| Sat 16 May (eve) | Arrive · North Bund/Hongkou panorama · Zhapu Road street food dinner |
| Sun 17 May | 1933 Old Millfun → Jewish Refugees Museum → M50 art park & Suzhou Creek → Jing'an Temple → local dinner (Fu 1039 / Julu Road) |
| Mon 19 May (morn) | Local breakfast · brief Bund walk · depart 09:30 for PVG |

---

### Saturday 16 May -- Arrival Day

**Afternoon (arrive hotel ~15:00-16:00)**

- Check in and drop bags.
- Rather than the main Bund promenade, cross **Waibaidu Bridge** north to the **Hongkou side** (North Bund area). This quieter bank of the Suzhou Creek mouth gives a simultaneous panorama of the historic Bund buildings behind you and the Lujiazui skyline across the river -- with a fraction of the tourist crowd.
- Walk south on **Sichuan North Road** -- a working local commercial street where the tourist veneer disappears entirely. Good for people-watching and roadside snacks: jian bing (savoury crepes), sesame flatbreads, and grilled skewers from pavement vendors.

**Evening**

- Return to the Bund for sunset -- even on a local-focused itinerary, the golden-hour and evening light on Lujiazui from the Bund side is the defining Shanghai view and should not be skipped.
- **Dinner: Zhapu Road Food Street (Zhapu Lu), Hongkou District** -- a few blocks north of the Bund, one of Shanghai's earliest and most authentic food streets. Shanghainese restaurants, seafood, braised pork belly, drunken chicken, and hand-pulled noodles. Inexpensive and genuinely local. Little English on most menus; pointing at neighbouring tables' dishes works fine.

---

### Sunday 17 May -- Full Day

**Morning: Hongkou District -- 1933 Old Millfun and Jewish Quarter (09:00-12:00)**

- Metro **Line 12** to **Tian'ai Road station**, walk ~10 min southeast to **1933 Old Millfun (1933 Laochang Fang)**, Shajing Road.
  - A 1933 Art Deco and Bauhaus-influenced former slaughterhouse converted into a creative hub: concrete spiral ramps, bridge walkways between buildings, art galleries, and independent cafes. One of the most architecturally striking spaces in Shanghai, and almost completely missed by package tours. Entry to the building is free; some exhibitions charge ~10 RMB. Allow 1-1.5 hrs.
- Walk ~15 min north to the **Shanghai Jewish Refugees Museum**, 62 Changyang Road.
  - Documents the story of 20,000+ European Jewish refugees who sheltered in the Hongkou ghetto during WWII -- a chapter of history unique to Shanghai. Small, serious, and moving. Entry ~50 RMB. Allow about 1 hr.

**Midday: M50 Art District and Suzhou Creek (12:30-15:30)**

- Metro Lines 3 or 4 to **Zhongtan Road station**, walk ~10 min east to **M50 Creative Park (50 Moganshan Road)**, along the Suzhou Creek waterfront.
  - Former 1930s-50s cotton mill warehouses converted into contemporary galleries and working artist studios. Over 100 open gallery and studio spaces, most free to enter. Shanghai's equivalent of Beijing's 798 art district -- smaller, less commercialised, more genuine. Good for casual browsing with no set agenda.
  - Walk the **Suzhou Creek towpath** beside M50 -- industrial warehouse conversions, local fishermen, old cargo boats, and a completely different atmosphere from the tourist-heavy central Bund area.
- **Lunch near M50:** Small noodle shops and local restaurants in the streets around Moganshan Road. A solid lunch here runs well under 50 RMB per person.

**Afternoon: Jing'an Local Neighbourhood (15:30-18:30)**

- Metro **Line 2 or Line 7** to **Jing'an Temple station**.
- **Jing'an Temple:** A gold-roofed working Buddhist temple incongruously surrounded by luxury malls on Nanjing West Road -- a characteristically Shanghai juxtaposition. Entry ~50 RMB. An active place of worship, not a tourist set piece.
- **Wujiang Road Food Street:** Half a block from the temple. Look for **Xiao Yang Sheng Jian (Small Yang's Pan-Fried Buns)** -- the original location of Shanghai's famous shengjian bao (pan-fried pork buns with crispy bases and a burst of broth inside). Queue moves fast; eat standing on the street. Outstanding and very cheap.
- Walk west along **Kangding Road and Yanping Road** -- entirely local residential streets with fruit stalls, wet market sections, retired residents, and corner breakfast shops still serving mid-afternoon. A 30-min wander here shows a Shanghai that has changed little in daily rhythm.

**Evening: Sinan Road and Fuxing Park (19:00 onwards)**

- Metro Line 1 to **South Shaanxi Road station**, walk east along Fuxing Road.
- **Sinan Road (Sinan Lu):** A quiet, elegant street of 1920s French villas converted to small restaurants and boutiques. Less visited than nearby Wukang Road and with a more genuine neighbourhood feel.
- **Fuxing Park:** The closest Shanghai gets to a Parisian neighbourhood park -- plane trees, elderly couples dancing to old Shanghai pop music, tai chi at dusk, men with birdcages. Free entry; open until around 21:00.
- **Dinner:** The streets around Sinan Road and Xinle Road have excellent small restaurants. The lanes around Julu Road reward spontaneous exploration. For something more planned, **Fu 1039** (1039 Yuyuan Road, Changning District) is a well-regarded Shanghainese restaurant in a restored villa -- reserve a table a day ahead.
- **Late drink:** Walk to **Anfu Road** (~10 min from Fuxing Park) for the bar and live music strip. JZ Club for jazz; various small bars with outdoor seating when the weather holds.

---

### Monday 19 May -- Morning Departure

**Leave hotel by 09:30. Keep this very light.**

- **Early local breakfast (07:30-08:30):** Find a neighbourhood shop near the hotel for a local send-off. Wonton soup (hun tun mian), scallion oil noodles (cong you ban mian), or plain congee with pickles are classic Shanghai morning staples. Any small shop with plastic stools and steam rising from the kitchen is a good sign. Typically under 20 RMB per person.
- **Brief walk (08:30-09:00):** The Bund at this hour is quiet and has entirely different light from the evening -- a good final 15 minutes.
- **09:30 depart for PVG.**
  - **By DiDi (recommended for luggage):** Book via the DiDi app (international Visa/Mastercard supported). ~45-70 min to PVG depending on Monday morning traffic. Consider booking the night before.
  - **By Metro plus Maglev:** Metro Line 2 east to Longyang Road (~40-45 min), then the **Shanghai Maglev** to PVG (~8 min, 50 RMB per person). Efficient if travelling light.
- Aim to be at PVG check-in by 10:00 for a 12:05 international departure. Check-in cutoff is 60 min before departure (hard cutoff 11:05). Immigration and security can take 20-40 min on busy Monday mornings -- do not cut this close.
