# Xi'an — 2 nights (Mon 25 – Wed 27 May 2026)

## Recommendations

### 1. Holiday Inn Express Xi'an Bell Tower
- **Stars:** 4★
- **Area:** Inside the city walls, ~5 min walk to the Bell Tower and its metro station
- **Approx. price/night:** £38 (~$48 USD) — recent average, free breakfast included
- **Why:** Best-reviewed mid-range option in the area (9.2/10 on Trip.com, 2,800+ reviews; TripAdvisor #62 in Shaanxi). Reliable IHG standards, free breakfast, and a location you can't beat for the price.
- **Book:** https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/holiday-inn-express-xi-an-bell-tower.html?checkin=2026-05-25&checkout=2026-05-26&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1

### 2. Jinmao Hotel Xi'an Downtown (Bell & Drum Tower)
- **Stars:** 4★
- **Area:** Between the Bell Tower and Drum Tower, ~1 km walk to Muslim Quarter
- **Approx. price/night:** £33 (~$41–51 USD) — often the cheapest 4★ in the zone
- **Why:** Rock-bottom rate for a 4-star right in the heart of the old city. Guests consistently praise the location, cleanliness, and spacious rooms; leaves budget room for food/experiences (breakfast is basic — skip it and eat in the Muslim Quarter).
- **Book:** https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/xi-an-gu-lou-jin-mao-jiu-dian-xi-39-an.html?checkin=2026-05-25&checkout=2026-05-26&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1

### 3. Eastern House Xi'an (Near Bell & Drum Tower City Wall)
- **Stars:** 4★ boutique
- **Area:** Inside the city walls, ~5 min walk to City Wall, 12 min to Drum Tower, 0.4 mi (~7 min) to Muslim Quarter
- **Approx. price/night:** £44–50 (~$56–64 USD)
- **Why:** Boutique feel with large, tastefully decorated rooms, rich breakfast buffet, and free afternoon tea — top-rated on Trip.com (5,600+ reviews). Closest of the three to the Muslim Quarter food scene.
- **Book:** https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/xi-an-dong-she-jiu-dian.html?checkin=2026-05-25&checkout=2026-05-26&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1

## Notes
May is peak season in Xi'an (warm, dry weather + pre-summer domestic tourism), so rates above are current indicative lows — expect 15–30% uplift for late-May weekday stays, and book early. A Chinese public holiday around late May can push prices higher still; check the exact dates when booking. Tip: Trip.com (Ctrip) often beats Booking.com on Chinese domestic properties — compare both before locking in. For all three hotels, a room facing the inner courtyard/side street is quieter than one facing the main road.
