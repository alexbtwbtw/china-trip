# Xi'an — Cool & Unique Stays (3 nights, Sun 24 – Wed 27 May 2026)

> 2 rooms, 3 nights. Budget target: £45–65/room/night (£90–130/night total). Stretch to £100/room for exceptional. Rate estimates converted from USD at ~1.26 USD/GBP and from CNY at ~0.11 CNY/GBP (April 2026 approximate). Verify live prices before booking — May is shoulder-to-peak season in Xi'an.

### 1. Sofitel Legend People's Grand Hotel Xi'an — "The State Guesthouse Where Zhou Enlai and Queen Elizabeth Once Slept"

- **Stars:** 5★ (Michelin Guide hotel; Historic Hotels Worldwide member)
- **Area:** Xincheng District, inside the Ming Dynasty city walls — east quarter, 1 km from Bell Tower
- **Price/room/night:** ~£155–190 (Luxury Room / Premier Suite; standard entry is a 61 m² Premier Suite — this hotel has no basic rooms)
- **Total for 2 rooms, 3 nights:** ~£930–1,140 *(the splurge pick — see Notes)*
- **Why it's cool:** Built in 1953 as Xi'an's official State Guesthouse, this 71-room property is the only Sofitel Legend in mainland China. The low-rise building fuses French Art Deco detailing (fleur-de-lis ironwork, lofty ceilinged salons) with Sino-Soviet grandeur and is set in 40,000 m² of gardens containing more than 10,000 trees. Every room comes with 24-hour dedicated butler service. The hotel's private museum displays artefacts from the building's diplomatic history, and guests have included Henry Kissinger, François Mitterrand, and Queen Elizabeth II. China Condé Nast Traveler awarded it Best Room Design; it ranks #19 of 1,226 Xi'an hotels on TripAdvisor.
- **Practical:** 10-minute walk to Bell Tower; Metro Line 1 (Wulukou Station) is a 10-minute walk. The property is on Dongxin Street, slightly east of the city-centre cluster — a short taxi or e-bike ride to the Muslim Quarter. No caveats on quality; the only caveat is price.
- **Book:** [booking.com/hotel/cn/sofitel-legend-peoples-xian](https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/sofitel-legend-peoples-xian.html?checkin=2026-05-24&checkout=2026-05-27&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1)

### 2. Mehood Elegant Hotel Xi'an Bell Tower Yongningmen — "Tang Dynasty Fantasy, Rooftop City Wall Sundowners Included"

- **Stars:** 5★
- **Area:** Beilin District — inside the ancient city walls, directly adjacent to Yongningmen (South Gate / Nanmen), the most photogenic gate of the wall
- **Price/room/night:** ~£55–90 (Elegant King Room with City Wall View, 36–40 m²)
- **Total for 2 rooms, 3 nights:** ~£330–540
- **Why it's cool:** Every design decision in this hotel draws on the Tang Dynasty — layered glazed-tile motifs, imperial colour palettes, and an atmosphere described as "a new realm of the Tang." All rooms exceed 38 m² (unusually generous for a Chinese city-centre hotel). The headline feature is the **rooftop bar and terrace**: at night, guests look straight out at the illuminated South Gate and the ancient wall — one of the most cinematic views in Xi'an. The hotel also offers Hanfu (traditional Tang-dynasty clothing) rental for guests who want to photograph themselves against the wall. It sits at No. 2 South Street, metres from Yongningmen metro station and the city wall walkway.
- **Practical:** 1.2 km to Bell Tower (~15 min walk or 5 min metro); Yongningmen Metro Station (Lines 2 & 14) 460 m. Muslim Quarter is 1.3 km north. The rooftop bar can be crowded on weekends — book a rooftop table in advance.
- **Book:** [booking.com/hotel/cn/xi-an-zhong-lou-yong-zhu-men-ya-zhi-jiu-dian](https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/xi-an-zhong-lou-yong-zhu-men-ya-zhi-jiu-dian.html?checkin=2026-05-24&checkout=2026-05-27&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1)

### 3. Eastern House Boutique Hotel — "Calm Oriental Courtyard, 400 m from the Muslim Quarter Chaos"

- **Stars:** 4★ (Travelers' Choice; rated 9.1/10 on Booking.com from 1,042 reviews; #26 of 1,226 Xi'an hotels on TripAdvisor)
- **Area:** Beilin District — south of Bell Tower, at the crossing of Dachejia Alley and Fen Alley, inside the walls
- **Price/room/night:** ~£35–52 (Deluxe Queen Room; standard entry level)
- **Total for 2 rooms, 3 nights:** ~£210–312
- **Why it's cool:** This is the best-value "actually memorable" hotel in the walled city. The entire property is built around Eastern-Chinese aesthetics — dark wood screens, lantern-lit corridors, ink-wash art, and a serene courtyard feel that insulates you from the street noise outside. The hotel includes **complimentary afternoon tea** (served daily), free minibar snacks, and unlimited bottled water — rare touches at this price point. It is the hotel closest to both the South Gate (Nanmen, 5-minute walk) and the Muslim Quarter (400 m). Staff are consistently praised in reviews for producing handwritten notes, arranging transport, and going beyond the script. Rooms are spacious by Chinese boutique standards and soundproofed well.
- **Practical:** 650 m to Bell Tower; 400 m to Drum Tower; Yongningmen Metro (Lines 2 & 14) ~800 m. Some guests have noted room temperature control can be inconsistent in the heating/cooling switchover period — May should be fine. Free self-service laundry on multiple floors.
- **Book:** [booking.com/hotel/cn/xi-an-dong-she-jiu-dian](https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/xi-an-dong-she-jiu-dian.html?checkin=2026-05-24&checkout=2026-05-27&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1)

### 4. Tang Dynasty Art Garden Hotel — "Three Courtyards, Red Lanterns, Goldfish Ponds — and the Giant Pagoda Next Door"

- **Stars:** 4★ (9.5/10 on Trip.com; #63 of 1,226 Xi'an hotels on TripAdvisor)
- **Area:** Yanta District (Qujiang Cultural Zone) — adjacent to the Giant Wild Goose Pagoda, 4 km south of Bell Tower
- **Price/room/night:** ~£55–78 (Courtyard-View Double Room, 20 m²; Suite from ~£95)
- **Total for 2 rooms, 3 nights:** ~£330–468
- **Why it's cool:** This is the most authentically architectural hotel on this list. The property is arranged across three separate Ming/Qing-style courtyards separated by moon-gate doorways, bamboo groves, goldfish ponds, and stone lanterns. Rooms are furnished with traditional Chinese rosewood furniture; one reviewer wrote it "felt like staying in the house of a Tang dynasty poet." Evening guqin (ancient zither) performances are occasionally held in the main courtyard. The hotel is directly next to the Giant Wild Goose Pagoda — Xi'an's most iconic Tang Dynasty monument — and the adjoining Tang Paradise cultural park, meaning you can walk out the front door into the 7th century. The building is architecturally genuine rather than themed-hotel pastiche.
- **Practical:** Located in Qujiang (Furong East Road), 4 km from Bell Tower — best reached by metro (Dayanta Station, Line 3, 430 m from hotel) or taxi (~¥20). A deliberate trade-off: quieter, more cultural, less convenient for the Muslim Quarter. Taxi drivers sometimes struggle to find it — save the Chinese address (西安唐苑艺术酒店, 雁塔区芙蓉东路6-1号). Breakfast (CNY 98/person) is praised for quality but not included in base rate.
- **Book:** [booking.com/hotel/cn/tang-dynasty-art-garden](https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/tang-dynasty-art-garden.html?checkin=2026-05-24&checkout=2026-05-27&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1)

### 5. Grand Mercure Xi'an on Renmin Square — "1957 Sino-Soviet Landmark, Gardens Inside the Ancient Walls"

- **Stars:** 5★ (rated 9.5/10 on Trip.com from 325 reviews)
- **Area:** Xincheng District — inside the Ming Dynasty city walls, on Renmin Square (same address block as the Sofitel Legend, its more famous neighbour)
- **Price/room/night:** ~£30–55 (Superior Room, 30 m²; Deluxe Suite from ~£80)
- **Total for 2 rooms, 3 nights:** ~£180–330
- **Why it's cool:** In 1957, Xi'an built this low-rise complex as a prestige civic guesthouse in the Sino-Soviet architectural style then fashionable across China — broad symmetrical facades, sweeping stairwells, imperial-scale public rooms. The building survived the reform era intact, and Accor converted it as the Grand Mercure, preserving the Soviet-era bones while fitting it with traditional Oriental interiors: dark lacquer screens, ink-painted silk panels, and furniture that nods to the Tang and Han dynasties. The grounds are remarkable: ancient trees, fountain courtyards, and manicured gardens covering thousands of square metres — genuinely peaceful inside the city walls. The hotel is a 5-minute walk from its sibling the Sofitel Legend, so guests get the aesthetic hit of the heritage complex at roughly a third of the price.
- **Practical:** ~1 km to Bell Tower; Wulukou Station (Metro Line 1) ~10 min walk. Less prominent location on restaurant reviews — the hotel's own Le Jinlong restaurant is recommended. A slightly longer walk to the Muslim Quarter than hotels 2 and 3, but still well inside the walls.
- **Book:** [booking.com/hotel/cn/grand-on-renmin-square](https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/grand-on-renmin-square.html?checkin=2026-05-24&checkout=2026-05-27&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1)

## Notes

**Top pick if money is no object:** Sofitel Legend People's Grand Hotel Xi'an (Hotel 1). There is no other hotel in Xi'an that combines this level of genuine historical significance (1953 state guesthouse, diplomatic guest list, only Sofitel Legend in mainland China), architectural individuality (French-Soviet-Chinese fusion), and intimate scale (71 rooms, butler service for every room). It is a place with a story — not just a luxury box.

**Best-value "cool" pick under £60/room/night:** Eastern House Boutique Hotel (Hotel 3). At £35–52/room/night it is genuinely affordable, yet it offers a carefully designed Oriental boutique experience, an unbeatable position between the South Gate and the Muslim Quarter, complimentary afternoon tea, and the kind of staff attentiveness usually reserved for properties twice the price. For a heritage-flavoured Xi'an stay without stretching the budget, this is the clear winner.

**Note on Hotel 4 (Tang Dynasty Art Garden):** This property is in a different part of the city (Qujiang, near the Pagoda) and is better suited to a trip focused on the Tang cultural zone rather than the old walled city centre. Include it if the itinerary will be split between the wall/Muslim Quarter area and the Pagoda/Tang Paradise area.
