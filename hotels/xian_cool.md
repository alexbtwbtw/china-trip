### 1. Eastern House Boutique Hotel — "Calm Oriental Courtyard, 400 m from the Muslim Quarter Chaos"

- **Stars:** 4★ (Travelers' Choice; rated 9.1/10 on Booking.com from 1,042 reviews; #26 of 1,226 Xi'an hotels on TripAdvisor)
- **Area:** Beilin District — south of Bell Tower, at the crossing of Dachejia Alley and Fen Alley, inside the walls
- **Price/room/night:** ~£35–52
- **Total for 2 rooms:** ~£70–104/night
- **Why it's cool:** The best-value "actually memorable" hotel in the walled city. Built around Eastern-Chinese aesthetics — dark wood screens, lantern-lit corridors, ink-wash art, and a serene courtyard feel. Includes **complimentary afternoon tea** (daily), free minibar snacks, and unlimited bottled water. Closest hotel to both the South Gate (5-minute walk) and the Muslim Quarter (400 m). Staff consistently praised for handwritten notes and going beyond the script. Soundproofed well. Free self-service laundry on multiple floors.
- **Practical:** 650 m to Bell Tower; 400 m to Drum Tower; Yongningmen Metro (Lines 2 & 14) ~800 m.

### 2. Grand Mercure Xi'an on Renmin Square — "1957 Sino-Soviet Landmark, Gardens Inside the Ancient Walls"

- **Stars:** 5★ (rated 9.5/10 on Trip.com from 325 reviews)
- **Area:** Xincheng District — inside the Ming Dynasty city walls, on Renmin Square
- **Price/room/night:** ~£30–55 (Superior Room, 30 m²; Deluxe Suite from ~£80)
- **Total for 2 rooms:** ~£60–110/night
- **Why it's cool:** Built in 1957 as a prestige civic guesthouse in the Sino-Soviet architectural style — broad symmetrical facades, sweeping stairwells, imperial-scale public rooms. Accor converted it as the Grand Mercure, preserving the Soviet-era bones while fitting traditional Oriental interiors: dark lacquer screens, ink-painted silk panels. The grounds are remarkable: ancient trees, fountain courtyards, thousands of square metres of garden — genuinely peaceful inside the city walls. The aesthetic hit of a heritage complex at roughly a third of the Sofitel Legend's price next door.
- **Practical:** ~1 km to Bell Tower; Wulukou Station (Metro Line 1) ~10 min walk. Slightly longer walk to the Muslim Quarter than Hotels 1 and 3 but still well inside the walls.
- **Book:** [booking.com/hotel/cn/grand-on-renmin-square](https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/grand-on-renmin-square.html?checkin=2026-05-24&checkout=2026-05-27&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1)

### 3. Bell Tower Hotel — "Forty Years Facing the Bell Tower — Xi'an's Most Storied Address"

- **Stars:** 4★ (rated 8.9/10 from 3,344 reviews on Trip.com; ranked #55 of 1,226 Xi'an hotels on TripAdvisor)
- **Area:** Beilin District — 110 m from Xi'an Bell Tower, at the literal centre of the ancient walled city
- **Price/room/night:** ~£29–42
- **Total for 2 rooms:** ~£58–84/night
- **Why it's cool:** Founded in 1983 — the hotel that has faced the Bell Tower longer than any other building on the block. A Deluxe Bell Tower Panoramic Family Room (45 m², two double beds, direct wall-to-wall view of the tower) and standard rooms from ~£29/night make this the most affordable way to wake up with Xi'an's most iconic monument framed in your window. The long-serving staff and function as a local meeting point since the 1980s give it a flavour no recently opened hotel can replicate.
- **Practical:** Zhonglou Metro Station (Lines 2 & 6) is 50 m from the front door. Muslim Quarter 0.5 km; Drum Tower 0.3 km; South Gate 1.2 km. Bell Tower View rooms sell out at peak summer weekends — book early. The hotel's fabric is dated — it is not a design hotel — but the views and location are unmatched at this price.

### 8. Tang Dynasty Art Garden Hotel — "Three Courtyards, Red Lanterns, Goldfish Ponds — and the Giant Pagoda Next Door"

- **Stars:** 4★ (9.5/10 on Trip.com; #63 of 1,226 Xi'an hotels on TripAdvisor)
- **Area:** Yanta District (Qujiang Cultural Zone) — adjacent to the Giant Wild Goose Pagoda, 4 km south of Bell Tower

Three separate Ming/Qing-style courtyards separated by moon-gate doorways, bamboo groves, goldfish ponds, and stone lanterns. Rooms furnished with traditional Chinese rosewood furniture. ### 9. Hyatt Centric Gaoxin Xi'an — "Kengo Kuma's Silk Façade: Where the Silk Road Begins Again"

- **Stars:** 5★ (rated 9.6/10 from 1,132 reviews on Trip.com; Hyatt Centric brand)
- **Area:** Yanta District (Xi'an Hi-Tech Zone / Gaoxin) — modern business district south of the ancient walls; 11 km to Bell Tower
- **Price/room/night:** ~£58–82 (entry Deluxe King/Twin ~£58–65; Suites from ~£105)
- **Total for 2 rooms:** ~£116–164/night
- **Why it's cool:** The only hotel in Xi'an designed by a Pritzker Prize-winning architect. Kengo Kuma's concept "Woven Silk" — the entire façade clad in multi-layered anodised aluminium louvers woven to replicate the shimmer of silk fabric, glowing like a lantern at night. Rooms have warm bamboo-green wallpaper, natural wood tones, floor-to-ceiling windows. Opened late 2023. The concept is not superficial: it references Xi'an's identity as the Silk Road origin point with genuine architectural commitment.
- **Practical:** Metro Line 3 (Gaoxin Station) connects to downtown in ~30 min. Bell Tower is 11 km — best suited if the itinerary includes the Hi-Tech Zone or Giant Pagoda area. Entry-level rooms hit the budget; suites do not.
- **Book:** [booking.com/hotel/cn/hyatt-centric-gaoxin-xi-an](https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/hyatt-centric-gaoxin-xi-an.html?checkin=2026-05-24&checkout=2026-05-27&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1)
