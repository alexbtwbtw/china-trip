## Recommendations

### 1. Mercure Chongqing Jiefangbei Hongya Cave
- **Stars:** 4★ (Accor ALL)
- **Area:** Yuzhong — No. 128-132 Minzu Road, sitting **directly above the Hongyadong scenic area** (~220 m to Hongya Cave entrance, ~7-min walk / 650 m to Jiefangbei Pedestrian Street monument); Xiaoshizi metro ~350 m
- **Approx. price/night per room:** £40–£50 (~$50–$62 / ~360–450 RMB) for a standard king/twin; "from" prices sometimes dip lower but £45 is a realistic mid-May weekday rate
- **Room type shortlisted:** Superior Twin Room / Superior King Room (both listed as the base "standard" tier)
- **Review score:** 9.6/10 Trip.com (2,528 reviews) — highest-rated of this shortlist
- **Connecting/adjacent rooms available?** Likely yes — 300+ room Accor property with a mix of king/twin configurations; connecting-rooms request is standard Accor practice but not guaranteed. Flag "two rooms, same floor, adjacent if possible" in the booking special-requests box or follow up by email with the property.
- **Why:** The single most central 4★ on this list — you physically walk out of the lift into Hongyadong. International-brand quality control (Accor), 9.6/10 across 2,528 reviews, and it consistently sits in the £40–£50 band for shoulder-season weekdays. Booking two identical superior rooms is trivial given the inventory size.
- **Book:** https://us.trip.com/hotels/chongqing-hotel-detail-101052804/mercure-chongqing-jiefangbei-hongya-cave/?checkIn=2026-05-19&checkOut=2026-05-22

### 2. Mercure Hotel Chongqing Jiefangbei (Downtown / Bayi Road)
- **Stars:** 4★ (Accor ALL)
- **Area:** Yuzhong — No. 183 Bayi Road, literally **adjacent to Jiefangbei Pedestrian Street** (~250 m / 3-min walk to the Liberation Monument); **10 m to Jiaochangkou Metro Station** (Exit 9, Line 1 + Line 2)
- **Approx. price/night per room:** £38–£48 (~$48–$60 / ~350–430 RMB) for a standard twin/king; lead-in "from" rate has been seen as low as $22 but expect mid-£40s for May weekdays
- **Room type shortlisted:** Superior Twin / Superior King
- **Review score:** 9.4/10 Trip.com across **9,341 reviews** (the largest review volume on this shortlist, i.e. most reliable signal); 363-room Accor property
- **Connecting/adjacent rooms available?** Yes — with 363 rooms and a deep twin/king mix, two-rooms-side-by-side is one of the easiest configurations in Jiefangbei. Email the property after booking with both reservation numbers and request connecting or adjacent rooms.
- **Why:** The closest metro access of any 4★ in this bracket, the biggest review sample (9,341 reviews) so guest score is statistically rock-solid, and international-brand standards for two travellers in separate rooms. Strongest value pick.
- **Book:** https://us.trip.com/hotels/chongqing-hotel-detail-29862594/mercure-hotel-chongqing-jiefangbei/?checkIn=2026-05-19&checkOut=2026-05-22

## Notes

- **Strongest value picks (top 3):** **Mercure Chongqing Jiefangbei (Bayi Road)** is the single safest choice on price/location/review-volume. **Mercure Hongya Cave** is the best location-to-Hongyadong (literally above it) and highest guest score. **SSAW Boutique** is the best rooms-per-pound if the travellers prioritise newness of the fit-out over maximum central-ness. Atour and Bolianhui are equally good but edge slightly over budget / slightly further out respectively.
- **All 5 are verified 4★** on Trip.com and have review scores ≥9.3/10 across sample sizes of 2,500–9,300+ reviews, well above the ≥9.0 bar.
- **Booking two rooms:** All five properties have 250–360+ rooms each, so booking 2 separate rooms under different lead guests is trivial inventory-wise. Put both reservations under the same primary email, then message the property (via Trip.com "contact hotel" or Booking.com's post-booking messaging) 1–2 weeks before check-in to request "same floor, adjacent rooms if possible." Connecting-doors cannot be guaranteed at any of these without pre-confirmation.
- **Booking.com vs Trip.com:** For all Chinese domestic properties, Trip.com's RMB-native rate is usually 5–15% cheaper than Booking.com's USD-quoted rate for the same room. All five URLs above point to Trip.com — cross-check Booking.com only if you want the property's international-brand English-language support channel (meaningful for the two Mercures, less so for SSAW/Atour/Bolianhui).
- **Price caveat:** Rates quoted are "from" figures for a standard room, pulled April 2026. Mid-May is shoulder season in Chongqing (pre-summer humidity, post-May-holiday-week) so rates should hold close to these; the May Day golden week is 1–5 May and is already past by the 19th.
- **Chongqing topography reminder:** "5-min walk to Jiefangbei" routinely involves stairs/escalators. Mercure Bayi Road and SSAW are at plateau level (easy walks). Mercure Hongya Cave and Atour are on the cliff face at Hongyadong — you exit into the scenic area at one level and climb/escalator up to Jiefangbei. Bolianhui (Shibati) sits lower and involves a modest uphill climb to the monument.
- **Brands not shortlisted and why:** Novotel has no Yuzhong property (only airport). InterContinental Raffles City is 5★ and runs £150+/night, well over budget. ISEYA/Seya Panoramic is verified 4★ but prices at $148–165/night, outside the £55 ceiling. Hanting Jiefangbei Centre is only 3★. Chongqing Ruyi Hotel does not surface as a distinct 4★ property on Trip.com/Booking.com. Chonpines Jiefangbei Hongyadong is sub-4★ (mid-range chain). Howard Johnson Chongqing properties are all in Jiangbei / new-city, not walkable to Jiefangbei.
