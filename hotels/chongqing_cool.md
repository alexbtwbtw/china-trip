# Chongqing — cool / worth-the-splurge picks (3 nights Tue 19 – Fri 22 May 2026)

## Recommendations

### 1. Niccolo Chongqing — "Sky Pool on the 52nd floor, cantilevered over a 250 m skyline"
- **Stars:** 5★ (Langham Hospitality, GHA Discovery)
- **Area:** Jiangbeizui CBD — Tower 1 of Chongqing IFS, floors 52–62. Across the Jialing river from Yuzhong peninsula but with a direct postcard view back onto Chaotianmen, Hongyadong at night, and the two-river confluence. One metro stop to Jiefangbei.
- **Approx. price/night:** ~£150–£180 for Deluxe, ~£200–£230 for a Deluxe River View King (from ~$183 cheapest / ~$141 starting on KAYAK; confirm the "N3 Deluxe River View" room type at checkout to get the Yangtze-facing side)
- **Why it's cool:** This is the single most cinematic hotel view in Chongqing — a 20 m panoramic Sky Pool with a **perspex base cantilevered off the side of the tower**, over 200 m above ground, looking directly at the Chaotianmen confluence and Raffles City's crystal sky-bridge. The bar on 62 and the lobby on 52 were designed as photography sets; guests repeatedly describe it as "otherworldly / like a movie." Being in Jiangbeizui (not Yuzhong) is the feature, not the bug — you see the cyberpunk peninsula instead of being inside it.
- **Practical:** 1 stop on Metro Line 6 to Xiaoshizi (Hongyadong / Jiefangbei); 10-min drive / cable-car hop across the Jialing. Sky Pool, spa, three restaurants, IFS mall downstairs. Opened 2017, 252 rooms, 9.6/10 across 11,000+ reviews.
- **Book:** https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/zhong-qing-ni-yi-ge-luo-jiu-dian.html?checkin=2026-05-19&checkout=2026-05-22&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1 — or Trip.com: https://us.trip.com/hotels/chongqing-hotel-detail-11290129/niccolo-chongqing/?checkIn=2026-05-19&checkOut=2026-05-22

### 2. InterContinental Chongqing Raffles City by IHG — "Inside Moshe Safdie's crystal-sail tower at the tip of the peninsula"
- **Stars:** 5★
- **Area:** Yuzhong — Chaotianmen Square, literally at the tip of the peninsula where the Yangtze and Jialing rivers meet. Inside one of the four sail-shaped Safdie towers connected by The Crystal sky-bridge 250 m up.
- **Approx. price/night:** ~£155–£185 Classic Double City View (from ~$196 / taxes to ~$229 early 2026); Executive River View high floors typically ~£210–£260. MICHELIN Guide Key hotel.
- **Why it's cool:** It's the hotel **inside the Moshe Safdie building** — the Marina Bay Sands 2.0 of Chongqing, CapitaLand's single-largest China project (RMB 24 billion). Executive River View rooms on high floors look straight down onto Chaotianmen Plaza and out over both rivers. Guests can access The Crystal Exploration Deck / sky-bridge infinity pool (clubhouse access depends on room class). Opened 2020, 380 rooms, 9.6/10 on Trip.com across 9,000+ reviews.
- **Practical:** 2 min walk to Chaotianmen metro (Line 6/Ring); the Raffles City mall is underneath; ~15 min walk to Hongyadong along the Binjiang riverfront, ~20 min walk / 1 metro stop to Jiefangbei. Indoor pool, 5 restaurants, spa, free parking.
- **Book:** https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/intercontinental-hotels-chongqing-raffles-city.html?checkin=2026-05-19&checkout=2026-05-22&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1 — or Trip.com: https://us.trip.com/hotels/chongqing-hotel-detail-61905308/intercontinental-chongqing-raffles-city/?checkIn=2026-05-19&checkOut=2026-05-22

### 3. YOLO Eling Art Gallery Hotel (Erling No. 2 Factory) — "Sleep inside a 1930s Republic-era mint on a 270° cliff platform"
- **Stars:** 4★ boutique (self-declared; review scores put it well above category)
- **Area:** Yuzhong — Eling Park / Erling No. 2 Factory Cultural and Creative Park, on the clifftop ridge of the peninsula. 5 min walk from Liziba monorail-through-building, ~2 km / 25 min walk down to Hongyadong.
- **Approx. price/night:** ~£60–£90 (~RMB 500–800; from ~$83 on some nights, higher in peak) — the best-value "cool" pick under £100 by a wide margin.
- **Why it's cool:** The building was the **Central Mint of the Republic of China** (a 1930s banknote printing factory), preserved with weathered industrial exterior + loft-style art-museum interior. Paintings, sculpture and installations fill the hallways; the signature is a **270° cantilevered cliff viewing platform** looking over the whole Yuzhong peninsula skyline. Only 42 rooms, 9.5/10 across 3,000+ reviews, consistently in Chongqing's Instagrammable-hotel top-10 lists.
- **Practical:** Up on the Eling ridge — quieter than Jiefangbei, but Liziba monorail and Eling Park are on your doorstep. Taxi or Line 2 metro down to riverfront attractions. Garden, restaurant, hot tub, free parking; pet-friendly (RMB 100/stay).
- **Book:** https://www.booking.com/hotel/cn/yoloe-ling-mei-zhu-guan-jiu-dian.html?checkin=2026-05-19&checkout=2026-05-22&group_adults=1&no_rooms=1 — or Trip.com: https://us.trip.com/hotels/chongqing-hotel-detail-19067753/yolo-eling-art-gallery-hotel/?checkIn=2026-05-19&checkOut=2026-05-22

This is the room you photograph.

- **Stars:** 5★ (Hyatt's Unbound Collection — the design-forward one-off sibling to Park Hyatt)

## Notes

**Top pick if money were no object:** **Niccolo Chongqing** — the Sky Pool is the single image that captures Chongqing's cyberpunk/river identity better than anywhere else in the city, and the floor-to-ceiling river view from Jiangbeizui back across to Chaotianmen/Hongyadong is postcard-level. If the guest wants to *be inside* the iconic building rather than look at it, swap in **InterContinental Raffles City** for equivalent money — that's the Safdie sail-tower itself.

**Best-value "cool" pick under £100:** **YOLO Eling Art Gallery Hotel** — a preserved 1930s Republic-of-China mint on a clifftop with a 270° viewing platform, at roughly ⅓ the price of any of the sky-lobby luxury picks. Only 42 rooms, book early. It's also the only pick that offers a *historic-building + art-museum* angle instead of a *tall-tower view* angle, which makes it a genuinely different experience if paired with one of the sky hotels.

**Brief-fit checklist:**
- View-focused: Niccolo (#1), InterContinental Raffles City (#2), Seya Flagship (#4), Lixury (#5) — four of five
- Design/boutique: YOLO Eling (#3), Seya Flagship (#4)
- Luxury brand: Niccolo (Langham), InterContinental (IHG), Lixury (Hyatt Unbound)
- Under £100: YOLO Eling (#3)
- Splurge £180+: Niccolo River View, InterContinental Executive River View, Seya signature tub suite

**Pricing caveats:** All rates are indicative "from" prices pulled in April 2026 from KAYAK / Booking.com / Trip.com for the May 19–22 window. Chongqing mid-May is shoulder season (pre-summer, post-May Day holiday of 1–5 May), so rates tend to soften vs. the "from" quotes. Trip.com is usually cheaper than Booking.com for domestic Chinese luxury brands — worth checking both. Booking.com pages currently return a bot-challenge to automated checks but render normally in a browser.

**Location trade-off:** Chongqing's peninsula is *vertical*. Seya (58th floor), Lixury (58–69th floor) and YOLO Eling (ridge top) are all at height inside the city, not at river level — you see the city from inside it. Niccolo and InterContinental sit across / at the tip of the confluence and look *back at* the skyline — that's a different and arguably better photo. Mix one of each if booking multiple cities.
