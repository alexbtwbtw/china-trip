## Notes
- **Budget reality check:** Genuine 2-bedroom units in Yuzhong are scarce near £90/night. Somerset is the only international-brand option that plausibly sits at that price point; Voyul is the best-value independent. Ascott Raffles City is a significant upgrade that breaks the budget — kept as option 3 because the brief explicitly named it.
- **Pricing caveats:** Trip.com and Agoda consistently show lower RMB-native rates than Booking.com for domestic Chinese aparthotels — compare both before booking. 2-bedroom inventory is thin, so the "from" prices you see for studios/1-beds do NOT apply; always click through to the specific 2-bedroom room type.
- **Search params:** Set occupancy to **4 adults** (not 2) to force booking sites to surface 2-bedroom inventory — searching with 2 guests often hides the larger units.
- **Fraser Suites / Citadines:** Fraser Suites does NOT operate in Chongqing. Citadines has two properties in Chongqing (Gaoke Liangjiang, Taoyuanju) but both are ~12 km from Jiefangbei — not walkable, so excluded.
- **Connecting-rooms fallback:** If all three above are sold out or over budget, a second option is to book **two connecting rooms** at a 4★ like Walling Hotel Jiefangbei Hongyadong or Atour S Jiefangbei Hongyadong Riverview — this must be requested directly with the property (email after booking), is not guaranteed, and typically runs £70–£90 combined for the pair. Not a true 2-bedroom suite, but cheaper and more central.
- **Chongqing topography:** The peninsula is extremely vertical. "500 m to Jiefangbei" can involve long escalator/stair climbs from the riverfront. Somerset and Ascott Raffles are both at river level — factor in the climb up to the Liberation Monument plateau.
